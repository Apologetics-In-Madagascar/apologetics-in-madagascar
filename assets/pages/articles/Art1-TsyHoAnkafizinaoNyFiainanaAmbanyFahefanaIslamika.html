THE REALITY OF SHARIA: A SYSTEM OF TOTAL CONTROL

Raymond Ibrahim, historian and author of Sword and Scimitar (Da Capo Press, 2018), has observed:

"If Islam is the doctrinal blueprint of the faith, Sharia Law is the mandatory legal engine that enforces and upholds it in the physical world."

— Raymond Ibrahim, source on YouTube
ⓘ Note on Quranic verse references (English section): All verse quotations in this section use the Sahih International translation as available on QuranX.com, which also provides Arabic text, transliteration, classical Tafsir, and authenticated Hadith collections for independent verification. Verse numbering follows the standard QuranX / Sahih International numbering convention.

The following analysis examines Sharia — Islamic law derived from the Qur'an and authenticated Hadith literature — as a comprehensive legal and political system. The objective is not polemic but documentation: to present what classical Islamic jurisprudence prescribes, grounded in primary scriptural sources, and to assess its implications for individual liberty, civil pluralism, and the rights of non-Muslim populations living under its jurisdiction.

I. The Penal Code: Fixed Punishments and the Primacy of the Communal Order

Classical Sharia jurisprudence establishes a category of fixed penalties known as Hudud (lit. "limits"), prescribed directly by Quranic injunction and therefore considered non-negotiable by traditional Islamic legal scholars. These penalties govern offenses that Islamic law treats as crimes against God rather than merely against individuals or the state, and are held to be outside the jurisdiction of human mitigation.

The foundational premise is the doctrinal supremacy of the Ummah — the collective body of believers — over individual conscience. Qur'an 3:110 (QuranX) designates the Muslim community as: "the best nation produced [as an example] for mankind. You enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong and believe in Allah." Classical commentators, including Ibn Kathir, interpret this verse as a communal legal mandate, not merely a moral aspiration.

Qur'an 4:65 (QuranX) establishes that authentic faith is conditional on total submission to prophetic authority: "But no, by your Lord, they will not believe until they make you a judge in their disputes, then do not find within their hearts any dissent to your verdict and submit in full submission." The legal implications of this verse have been used across Islamic jurisprudence to ground the illegitimacy of dissent.

The prescribed physical penalties include: amputation of the hand for theft — "[As for] the thief, the male and the female, amputate their hands in recompense for what they committed as a deterrent [punishment] from Allah" (Q. 5:38, QuranX); one hundred lashes for fornication (Q. 24:2); and death for apostasy, established firmly in authenticated Hadith — Sahih Bukhari 6922 records: "Whoever changed his Islamic religion, then kill him."

II. Theological and Political Enforcement: The Legal Architecture of Submission

Beyond the penal code, Sharia constructs a comprehensive political theology that regulates the relationship between the believer, the state, and non-Muslim populations.

Qur'an 33:36 (QuranX) states: "It is not for a believing man or a believing woman, when Allah and His Messenger have decided a matter, that they should [thereafter] have any choice about their affair." Classical jurisprudence applied this broadly to prohibit independent legal reasoning on matters deemed settled by prophetic precedent.

The prohibition on protective alliances with non-Muslims (Q. 4:89) has been applied historically to restrict the civic participation of Muslim minorities in pluralist societies, and to justify the exclusion of non-Muslim populations from positions of authority in Islamic states.

Freedom of expression regarding Islamic doctrine is constrained by Q. 33:57 (QuranX): "Indeed, those who abuse Allah and His Messenger — Allah has cursed them in this world and the Hereafter and prepared for them a humiliating punishment." Hadith Sunan Abu Dawood 4339 records a case in which the Prophet sanctioned the killing of a woman who had criticized him, without legal penalty for the perpetrator.

Gender inequality is codified at the level of legal testimony and inheritance: "And bring to witness two witnesses from among your men. And if there are not two men [available], then a man and two women from those whom you accept as witnesses — so that if one of the women errs, then the other can remind her." (Q. 2:282, QuranX). Female heirs receive half the share of male heirs in equivalent relationships (Q. 4:11).

III. Jihad and Territorial Expansion: The Doctrinal Mandate

Classical jurisprudence, including the major Sunni legal schools (madhabs), recognized armed Jihad as a collective religious obligation (fard kifaya) when Muslim territories were under threat, and in certain formulations as an expansionary duty directed at extending the domain of Islamic governance (Dar al-Islam) at the expense of the non-Islamic territory (Dar al-Harb).

The primary scriptural basis for combat prescriptions includes Q. 8:12 (QuranX): "I will cast terror into the hearts of those who disbelieved, so strike [them] upon the necks and strike from them every fingertip."

Qur'an 47:4 (QuranX) addresses the treatment of captives: "So when you meet those who disbelieve [in battle], strike [their] necks until, when you have inflicted slaughter upon them, then secure their bonds, and either [confer] favor afterwards or ransom [them] until the war lays down its burdens." The same verse permits the retention of prisoners as bonded captives — the scriptural foundation for the institution of slavery under Islamic law.

Qur'an 9:5 (QuranX) — known in classical exegesis as the "Verse of the Sword" — commands: "And when the sacred months have passed, then kill the polytheists wherever you find them and capture them and besiege them and sit in wait for them at every place of ambush." Ibn Kathir interprets this verse as abrogating earlier, more conciliatory passages.

Qur'an 9:29 (QuranX) specifically addresses the "People of the Book - Ahl al-Kitāb" (Christians and Jews): "Fight those who do not believe in Allah or in the Last Day and who do not consider unlawful what Allah and His Messenger have made unlawful and who do not adopt the religion of truth from those who were given the Scripture — [fight] until they give the jizyah willingly while they are humbled."

IV. Historical Application: The Institutional Record

From the campaigns initiated during the Prophet's lifetime through the successive Rashidun, Umayyad, Abbasid, and Ottoman caliphates, Islamic expansion was pursued through a combination of military conquest, economic subjugation via the Jizya system, and demographic transformation. The execution of apostates (riddah) was enforced from the earliest period — the Wars of Apostasy (Ridda Wars) under Caliph Abu Bakr (632–633 AD) being the first major instance — and remained codified in classical Islamic law across all major legal schools.

The Pact of Umar (c. 637 AD), named after the second Rashidun Caliph : Umar ibn al-Khattab, established the formal legal framework governing non-Muslim subjects (dhimmis) under Islamic rule. Its conditions included the payment of the jizya, prohibition on constructing new houses of worship, or repairing the already existing ones, prohibition on public religious expression, and restrictions on dress and civic participation. Populations outside the dhimmi classification faced the alternatives of conversion, enslavement, or death under Q. 9:5.

The institutionalization of slavery under Islamic law is grounded in Q. 23:5–6 and Q. 70:29–30, which permit sexual relations with "those whom your right hands possess." Q. 4:24 (QuranX) additionally stipulates: "And [also prohibited to you are all] married women except those your right hands possess." — rendering the previous marriages of captured women legally nullified upon their capture.

V. Marriage, Family Law, and the Guardianship System

Polygyny is explicitly permitted by Q. 4:3. Women's rights within the marriage contract are substantially asymmetric: the right of unilateral divorce (talaq) belongs exclusively to the husband under classical formulations.

Qur'an 4:34 (QuranX) addresses marital authority: "Men are in charge of women by [right of] what Allah has given one over the other and what they spend [for maintenance] from their wealth. So righteous women are devoutly obedient, guarding in [the husband's] absence what Allah would have them guard. But those [wives] from whom you fear arrogance — [first] advise them; [then if they persist], forsake them in bed; and [finally], strike them." The phrase rendered as "strike them" (wadribuhunna) has been the subject of exegetical debate; the majority of classical commentators, including Al-Tabari and Ibn Kathir, interpret it as permitting physical discipline.

Qur'an 65:4 (QuranX) refers in its divorce regulations to wives "who have not yet menstruated", establishing a legal framework applicable to pre-pubescent girls. Sahih Bukhari 5133 records that the Prophet's marriage to Aisha was contracted when she was six years of age and consummated when she was nine. Classical Islamic jurisprudence has cited this precedent in setting the minimum age for marriage.

VI. Conclusion: The Structural Implications for Human Liberty

The examination conducted in this article is textual and historical, not theological in the devotional sense. Its purpose is to document what classical Sharia prescribes — on the basis of the primary sources from which it derives — and to assess the structural consequences of those prescriptions for individual liberty, legal equality, and civil pluralism.

The evidence surveyed is consistent across sources: Sharia as classically formulated is a comprehensive legal system that subordinates individual conscience to communal religious authority, restricts the civic and legal standing of non-Muslim populations, codifies significant inequalities between men and women, and prescribes severe penalties for conduct — apostasy, blasphemy, sexual transgression — that most contemporary legal frameworks treat as matters of individual freedom. These are not marginal features of the system; they are documented in its foundational texts and applied across its institutional history.

To acknowledge these facts is not to condemn every Muslim individual, the majority of whom do not live under full Sharia governance and many of whom actively contest these provisions. It is, rather, to take seriously the textual and institutional content of a legal tradition that continues to shape governance, law, and social norms across a significant portion of the world's population — and to insist that honest examination of that content is a precondition for any meaningful engagement with it.

I. Ny Lalàna Momba ny Famaizana: Famaizana Raikitra sy ny Fahambonian'ny Vondrom-piarahamonina

II. Ny Fampiharana ara-Teolojia sy ara-Politika

Ny fitsipika klasika dia nampiasa ity andininy ity mba handraran'ny fanadihadiana mahaleotena amin'ny raharaha heverina ho efa voafaritry ny presedan'ny mpaminany.

Ny fandrarana ny fifanaraham-piarovana amin'ny tsy mpino (K. 4:90 = QuranX 4:89) dia nampiasaina mba hanerena ny fandraisan'anjara ara-sivily ny vitsy an'isa Silamo ao amin'ny fiarahamonina marolafy.

IV. Ny Fampiharana ara-Tantara

Hatramin'ny fanafihana natomboka tamin'ny andron'ny Mpaminany ka hatramin'ny Kalifà Rashidun, Umayyad, Abbasid, sy Ottoman nifandimby, ny fanitarana Islamika dia notanterahina tamin'ny fampifangaroan'ny fandresen ara-miaramila, ny fampanompoana ara-toekarena tamin'ny alalan'ny rafitry ny Jizya, ary ny fiovaovana demografika. Ny famonoana ny mpivadika amin'ny finoana (riddah) dia nampiharina hatramin'ny taonjato voalohany — ny Ady momba ny Fialana amin'ny Finoana (Ridda Wars) teo ambanin'i Califà Abu Bakr (632–633 AD) no tranga lehibe voalohany.

Ny Fifanekena nataon'i Umar [Ibn Al-Khattab, califà Rashidun faharoa] (c. 637 AD) dia nametraka ny rafitra ara-dalàna ofisialy mifehy ny olom-panjakana tsy Silamo (dhimmis): fandoavana ny Jizya, fandrarana ny fananganana tranom-pivavahana vaovao, na fanamboarna ny efa misy, fandrarana ny fanehoana ara-pivavahana ampahibemaso, ary fameperana ny fitafiana sy ny fandraisan'anjara ara-sivily. Ny mponina tsy anisan'ny sokajy dhimmi dia niatrika ny safidy telo: fiovana amin'ny finoana, fanandevozana, na fahafatesana (K. 9:6 = QuranX 9:5).

Ny fampidirana ny fanandevozana ao amin'ny lalàna Islamika dia miorina amin'ny K. 23:6–7 sy K. 70:30–31 (= QuranX 23:5–6; 70:29–30), izay mamela ny firaisana ara-nofo amin'ny "izay ananan'ny tananao havanana." Ny K. 4:25 (= QuranX 4:24) dia manampy fa ny fanambadian'ny vehivavy voasambotra teo aloha dia foana rehefa voasambotra izy ireo.

Ny ankamaroan'ny mpaneloselo klasika, anisan'izany i Al-Tabari sy Ibn Kathir, dia manazava ity andininy ity ho mamela ny fampianarana ara-batana.

VI. Fehiny: Ny Vokany Rafitra amin'ny Fahalalahana Olombelona

Ny fandalinana nataon'ity lahatsoratra ity dia ara-tsoratra sy ara-tantara. Ny tanjonany dia ny hanoratra izay voasoratra amin'ny Sharia klasika — miorina amin'ny loharano voalohany — ary ny handinika ny vokatry ny rafitra ireny amin'ny fahaleovantenan'ny tsirairay, ny fitovian'ny zo ara-dalàna, ary ny fiarahamonina marolafy.

Ny porofo nodinihina dia mifanaraka manerana ny loharano: ny Sharia araka ny fanazavana klasika dia rafitra ara-dalàna feno izay mametraka ny saina manokana eo ambanin'ny fahefan'ny ara-pivavahana vondrom-piarahamonina, mametra ny sata sivily sy ara-dalàna ny mponina tsy Silamo, manamafy tsy fitoviana lehibe eo amin'ny lahy sy ny vavy, ary manome famaizana mafy amin'ny fitondran-tena — fialana amin'ny finoana, fitenenan-dratsy an'ny Andriamanitra, fanelanelanana ara-nofo — izay heverinin'ny ankamaroan'ny rafitra ara-dalàna ankehitriny ho zo fanokana.

Ny fanekena ireto zava-misy ireto dia tsy fanamelohana ny Silamo tsirairay, izay ny ankamaroany dia tsy miaina eo ambanin'ny fitantanana Sharia feno ary maro amin'izy ireo no manohitra mivantana ireo fepetra ireo. Fa kosa, midika izany ny haka an-tsitrapo ny votoatin'ny lovam-pinoana ara-dalàna iray izay mbola miforma ny fitantanana sy ny fombafomba ara-tsosialy manerana ny ampahany lehibe amin'ny mponin'izao tontolo izao.
