Why Without The Church Militantism (readiness to defend the faith on both physical and spiritual fronts), Christianity Would Have Died A Long Time Ago

From the book Fear God and Take Your Own Part by Theodore Roosevelt (New York, George H. Doran Company, 1915), Chapter VI – “Peace Insurance by Preparedness Against War,” p. 196–197:

“Christianity is not the creed of Asia and Africa at this moment solely because the seventh century Christians of Asia and Africa, in addition to being rent asunder among themselves by bitter sectarian animosities—and sectarian intolerance and animosity stand for most that is evil in Christianity—had trained themselves not to fight, whereas the Moslems were trained to fight. Christianity was saved in Europe solely because the peoples of Europe fought. If the peoples of Europe in the seventh and eighth centuries, and on up to and including the seventeenth century, had not possessed a military equality with, and gradually a growing superiority over, the Mohammedans who invaded Europe, Europe would at this moment be Mohammedan, and the Christian religion would be exterminated. Wherever the Mohammedans have had complete sway, wherever the Christians have been unable to resist them by the sword, Christianity has ultimately disappeared. From the hammer of Charles Martel in the battle of Tours 732 A.D to the sword of Sobieski in the siege of Vienna in 1683, Christianity owed its safety in Europe to the fact that it was able to show that it could and would fight as well as the Mohammedans aggressors.”

I. The Uncomfortable Truth Modern Christians Avoid

Modern Christianity, especially in the West and under Western influence, suffers from historical amnesia. It speaks often of love, forgiveness, and turning the other cheek—yet forgets that it still exists because men once refused to surrender it.

Christianity was never merely a collection of private devotions. It was a civilization-forming faith, and civilizations do not survive on sentiment alone. The idea of the Church Militant—that Christians must be prepared to defend their faith both spiritually and physically—is not a medieval corruption of the Gospel. It is a historical necessity, as proven by the list below :

  1. The Crusades: A defensive necessity that preserved the survival of the christian faith against external conquest [in this case : Muslims's Jihadis Conquest].
  2. The Rule of Law and Human Rights: Rooted in the concept of Imago Dei (the Image of God), asserting that every individual has inherent dignity and value.
  3. The Birth of Modern Science: Developed by Christian thinkers who believed that a rational God created an orderly universe governed by discoverable laws.
  4. The University System: Established by the Church to preserve ancient knowledge and foster intellectual rigorousness, leading to the world's first higher learning institutions.
  5. Healthcare and Hospitals: A direct manifestation of Christian charity, creating the first public systems to care for the sick and the poor regardless of status.
  6. The Abolition of Slavery: A Christian moral imperative that led to the end of slavery in the Western world. [still practiced in Islamic/Mohammedans Countries]
  7. Art, Music, and Literature: The Church’s patronage of the arts led to some of humanity’s greatest cultural achievements, from Michelangelo’s Sistine Chapel to Jean Sebastian de Bach’s Mass in B Minor.

II. The Seventh-Century Catastrophe known as Islam

In the seventh century, vast Christian lands fell to Islamic Jihad Aggressive Conquests under Mohammed, the Rashidun and the Ummayad Caliphate with astonishing speed: Syria, Egypt, and North Africa. These were not pagan territories. They were deeply Christian—homes to ancient churches, theologians, monasteries, and councils.

As Roosevelt observed, Christians in those regions were divided by bitter theological quarrels and had largely lost the will—or the capacity—to defend themselves militarily. History is merciless toward the defenseless.

III. Europe’s Survival Was Not Accidental

Europe remained Christian because it fought. In 732, at the Battle of Tours, Charles Martel halted the northward advance of Islamic armies. Centuries later, in 1683, at the Battle of Vienna, John III Sobieski led a decisive charge with his Winged Hussars that preserved Central Europe from conquest.

IV. The Meaning of “Church Militant”

The term “Church Militant” does not glorify violence. It reflects a theological reality. The struggle is first spiritual:

  • resisting sin
  • heresy
  • corruption
  • falsehood
. But history demonstrates that it has sometimes also required physical defense.

V. Pacifism and Its Limits

Christianity teaches mercy; it does not command civilizational suicide. Passive resignation did not preserve faith communities where invaders destroyed churches and enslaved populations [Ottomans's Conquest of the Byzantine Empire and its expansions in the Balkans prior to the siege of Vienna in 1683]. Resistance did.

VI. The Madagascar Case: A Local Illustration of Conviction Under Fire

The lesson is not only European. In Madagascar, Christianity did not grow without resistance or struggle.

During the reign of Ranavalona I, Christians faced severe persecution. Converts were imprisoned, tortured, or executed for refusing to renounce their faith. Public worship was suppressed; Christian practice was driven underground.

Yet the faith did not disappear. Why?

Because Malagasy Christians were spiritually militant. They memorized Scripture when Bibles were confiscated. They gathered secretly. They endured suffering rather than apostatize.

"The blood and steadfastness of persecuted believers became the seed of growth."

When persecution subsided after 1861, Christianity did not re-emerge as a fragile remnant—it expanded dramatically. Madagascar today has a large Christian population not because the faith was left unchallenged, but because believers refused to abandon it under pressure.

Here, the Church Militant was not cavalry or swords—it was courage, resilience, and unbreakable conviction.

VII. The Spiritual Front: Just as Crucial

Militant Christianity is not merely about swords. It has survived because apologists defended doctrine, theologians refuted false teachings, and monks preserved texts. The Church Militant combines conviction with courage.

VIII. The Modern Crisis

Today, many assume Christianity is self-sustaining. It is not. Every generation either defends the faith—intellectually, morally, socially—or watches it erode. Militancy today requires intellectual preparedness, cultural clarity, and moral courage.

IX. The Historical Verdict

Roosevelt’s words echo a historical consensus: Civilizations survive when they possess both conviction and strength. The Church Militant was not a betrayal of Christianity; it was, at key moments, its shield and sword.

Conclusion: To acknowledge the necessity of Christian militancy is to recognize reality. A faith that refuses to defend itself invites extinction. Without the Church Militant, Christianity would have died long ago.

I. Ny Fahamarinana mampivarahontsana izay ialan'ny Kristianina ankehitriny

Ny Kristianisma ankehitriny, indrindra fa any Andrefana sy ireo eo ambanin'ny heriny, dia tratry ny fanadinoana ny tantara. Matetika isika miresaka momba ny fitiavana, famelan-keloka, ary ny fanolorana ny takolaka ilany—nefa adinontsika fa mbola velona ny finoana satria nisy lehilahy-miaramila tsy nety nilefitra tamin'ny famoretana.

Ny Kristianisma dia tsy fivavahana ho an'ny tsirairay fotsiny. Finoana namolavola sivilizasiona izy, ary tsy velona amin'ny fihetseham-po fotsiny ny sivilizasiona. Ny foto-kevitry ny "Fiangonana Mpiady" (Ecclesia Militans)—izay manambara fa tsy maintsy vonona hiaro ny finoany ny Kristianina na ara-panahy na ara-batana—dia tsy kolikoly tamin'ny Moyen Âge, fa filàna ara-tantara, araka ny hita amin'ireto ohatra ireto:

  1. Ny Kroazada (The Crusades): Hetsika fiarovana tena izay niantoka ny fahaveloman'ny finoana kristiana manoloana ny fanafihana sy fanitaran-tany nataon'ireo Jihadista silamo.
  2. Ny Fanjakan'ny Lalàna sy ny Zon'olombelona: Avy amin'ny foto-kevitra Imago Dei (ny endrik'Andriamanitra), manamafy fa ny olona tsirairay dia manana lanja sy hasina raiki-tampisaka.
  3. Ny Terak'ny Siansa maoderina: Novolavolain'ireo mpandinika kristiana izay nino fa Andriamanitra mahay mandanjalanja no namorona izao tontolo izao feno lamina sy fehezin'ny lalàna azo fikarohana.
  4. Ny Rafitra Oniversite: Naorin'ny Fiangonana mba hitehirizana ny fahalalana fahiny sy hanabeazana ny saina, izay niteraka ireo toeram-pampianarana ambony voalohany teto an-tany.
  5. Ny Fitsaboana sy ny Hopitaly: Vokatry ny fiantrana kristiana mivantana, namorona ny rafi-panjakana voalohany hikarakarana ny marary sy ny mahantra tsy anavahana saranga.
  6. Ny Fanafoanana ny Fanandevozana: Adidy ara-pitondrantena kristiana izay nitarika ho amin'ny fiafaran'ny fanandevozana tany amin'ny tontolo tandrefana (raha mbola nampiharina tany amin'ireo tany silamo izany).
  7. Ny Zavakanto, Mozika, ary Haivolana: Ny fanohanan'ny Fiangonana ny zavakanto dia niteraka sangan'asa lehibe indrindra eo amin'ny tantaran'ny olombelona, hatramin'ny trano fiangonana Sistine an'i Michelangelo ka hatramin'ny sangan'asan'i Jean Sebastian Bach.

II. Ny loza nitranga tamin'ny taonjato faha-7: Ny fipoiran'ny Silamo

Tamin'ny taonjato faha-7, firenena kristiana midadasika no lavon'ny fanafihana Jihadista mahery vaika teo ambany fitarihan'i Mohammed sy ny Kalifata (Caliphat) Rashidun ary Ummayad: ny tany Syria, Egypta, ary Afrika Avaratra. Tsy tany jentilisa ireo, fa tany tena kristiana — fonenan'ireo fiangonana fahiny, mpahay teolojia, ary monastera maro be.

Araka ny voalazan'i Roosevelt, ny Kristianina tany amin'ireo faritra ireo dia nizarazara noho ny fifandirana teo amin'ny samy teolojiana ary namoy ny finiavana — na ny fahaiza-manao — hiaro tena amin'ny fomba ara-miaramila. Ny tantara dia tsy mamindra fo amin'izay tsy mahay miaro tena.

III. Tsy kisendrasendra ny fahaveloman'i Eoropa

Nijanona ho kristiana i Eoropa satria niady. Tamin'ny taona 732, tany amin'ny ady tao Tours, nosakanan'i Charles Martel ny fandrosoan'ny tafika silamo nianavaratra Taonjato maro taty aoriana, tamin'ny taona 1683, tany amin'ny ady tao Vienne, i John III Sobieski sy ny "Winged Hussars" dia nitarika fanafihana mahery vaika izay namonjy an'i Eoropa Afovoany tsy ho voababon'ny Empira Ottoman notaritin'ny Vizir Kara Mustafa Pasha..

IV. Ny dikan'ny hoe “Fiangonana Mpiady”

Ny teny hoe “Fiangonana Mpiady” dia tsy natao hanandratana ny herisetra. Taratry ny zava-misy ara-teolojia izany. Ny ady dia ara-panahy voalohany:

  • fanoherana ny ota
  • fanoherana ny fampianaran-diso (heresy)
  • fanoherana ny kolikoly
  • fanoherana ny lainga
. Nefa ny tantara dia mampiseho fa indraindray izany dia nitaky fiarovana ara-batana ihany koa.

V. Ny fetran'ny Pasifisma (Tsy fanoherana)

Mampianatra famindram-po ny Kristianisma; saingy tsy mandidy ny hamonoana ny sivilizasiona intsika. Ny fipetrapetrahan-potsiny dia tsy niaro ny fiangonana tany amin'ny toerana izay nandravan'ny mpanafika ny trano fivavahana sy nanandevozana ny vahoaka. Ny fanoherana no nanao izany.

VI. Ny tranga teto Madagasikara: Ohatra teo an-toerana momba ny fijoroana amin'ny finoana

Tsy tany Eoropa ihany no nisy izany lesona izany. Teto Madagasikara koa, ny Kristianisma dia tsy nitombo raha tsy teo ny fanoherana sy ny tolona.

Tamin'ny andron'ny Mpanjakavavy Ranavalona I, ny Kristianina dia niatrika fanenjehana mahery vaika. Maro no nogadraina, nampijalina, na novonoina satria tsy nety niala tamin'ny finoany. Norarana ny fanompoam-pivavahana ampahibemaso, ka tany ambanin'ny tany no nifampivory ny mpino.

Nefa tsy nanjavona ny finoana. Nahoana?

Satria ny Kristianina malagasy dia mpino mpiady ara-panahy. Nianatra ny Soratra Masina izy ireo rehefa nogiazana ny Baiboly. Nivory mangingina izy ireo ary nifidy ny hijaly toy izay handà ny finoany.

"Ny ran'ny martiora sy ny fijoroan'ny mpino enjehina no nanjary voa nampitombo ny finoana."

Rehefa nitsahatra ny fanenjehana taorian'ny taona 1861, ny Kristianisma dia tsy nipoitra ho toy ny sisa kely malemy — fa nitefoka sy nitombo. Ny antony maha-kristiana ny maro an'isa eto Madagasikara ankehitriny dia tsy noho ny tsy fisian'ny sakana, fa noho ny mpino tsy nety niala tamin'ny finoany teo anatrehan'ny famoretana.

Teto, ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia tsy nampiasa an-tsabatra na tafika — fa nampiasa herim-po, faharetana, ary faharesen-dahatra tsy azo hozongozonina.

VII. Ny sehatra ara-panahy: Mitovy lanjany

Ny Kristianisma mpiady dia tsy resaka sabatra fotsiny. Tafika izy satria nisy ny mpandinika niaro ny fotopampianarana, nisy ny teolojiana nandà ny fampianaran-diso, ary nisy ny moanina nitehiry ny Soratra Masina. Ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia fitambaran'ny faharesen-dahatra sy ny herim-po.

VIII. Ny Krizy maoderina

Ankehitriny, maro no mihevitra fa mandeha ho azy ny faharetan'ny Kristianisma. Tsy izany anefa ny zava-misy. Ny taranaka tsirairay dia tsy maintsy miaro ny finoana — na amin'ny saina, na amin'ny fitondran-tena, na eo amin'ny fiaraha-monina. Ny "fanafihana" ankehitriny dia mitaky fahavononana ara-tsaina sy herim-po ara-pitondrantena.

IX. Ny Didim-pitsaran'ny Tantara

Ny tenin'i Roosevelt dia mampahatsiahy ny zava-misy: Ny sivilizasiona dia velona rehefa manana faharesen-dahatra sy hery. Ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia tsy famadihana ny Kristianisma; fa tamin'ny fotoana lehibe teo amin'ny tantara, dia izy no naha-ampinga sy sabatra niaro azy.

Fehiny: Ny fanekena ny ilàna ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia fanekena ny zava-misy. Ny finoana izay tsy vonona hiaro tena dia manasa ny fahafatesany. Raha tsy teo ny Fiangonana Mpiady, dia efa maty hatry ny ela ny Kristianisma.
