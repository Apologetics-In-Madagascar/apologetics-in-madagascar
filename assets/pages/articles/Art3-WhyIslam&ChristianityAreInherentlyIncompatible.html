WHY ISLAM AND CHRISTIANITY ARE INHERENTLY INCOMPATIBLE: FOURTEEN CENTURIES OF CIVILIZATIONAL COLLISION

This article is the third in a series. The first documented the structural content of Sharia as a comprehensive legal and political system. The second examined the theological and historical necessity of the Church Militant — the readiness of Christianity to defend itself on both the spiritual and physical fronts. This third article synthesizes both into the central argument that their incompatibility is not incidental, cultural, or political. It is structural, doctrinal, and permanent — and fourteen centuries of recorded history constitute the evidence.

I. The Thesis: Incompatibility Is Not an Accusation — It Is a Structural Description

The claim that Islam and Christianity are inherently incompatible is, in contemporary discourse, frequently treated as inflammatory. It is dismissed as the product of ignorance, political manipulation, or residual Western prejudice. This dismissal is itself a form of historical illiteracy — one that can only be sustained by refusing to examine what both traditions actually claim about themselves, about each other, and about the nature of legitimate political and spiritual authority.

The incompatibility is not a matter of cultural difference. Cultures can coexist, hybridize, and negotiate. It is not a matter of political rivalry. Political rivals can sign treaties and share borders in stable equilibrium. The incompatibility is doctrinal: both Islam and Christianity make universal, exclusive, and totalizing claims about the nature of God, the content of revelation, the legitimate ordering of human society, and the destiny of mankind. These claims are not merely different. At their core, they are mutually negating.

Christianity holds that Jesus Christ is the incarnate Son of God, that his death and resurrection constitute the definitive act of human redemption, and that salvation is available through faith in him. Islam explicitly and foundationally rejects each of these claims. The Quran declares that God has no son (Q. 19:35), that the crucifixion did not occur as Christians understand it (Q. 4:157), and that those who attribute partners to God — which classical Islamic jurisprudence applies to Trinitarian Christianity — commit the one unforgivable sin of shirk (Q. 4:48).

These are not peripheral disagreements. They concern the identity of God, the nature of salvation, and the validity of the entire Christian theological tradition. Islam does not merely disagree with Christianity. It formally declares Christianity, in its orthodox form, to be a corrupted deviation from the original monotheistic revelation — one that must ultimately yield, in any territory under Islamic governance, to the superior authority of Quranic law.

The history of the encounter between these two civilizations is, in this light, not a history of misunderstanding. It is a history of two systems behaving precisely according to their own internal logic — and the record of that encounter, across fourteen centuries and three continents, is what this article examines.

II. The First Clash: The Byzantine Empire and the Arab Conquests (634–641 AD)

The Battle of Ajnadayn, 634 AD

Within two years of the Prophet Muhammad's death in 632 AD, the armies of the nascent Islamic state had crossed into Byzantine Palestine. The first major engagement between Muslim and Christian forces occurred at Ajnadayn in July 634, where the Arab army under Khalid ibn al-Walid defeated a Byzantine force defending the southern frontier of the Levant. The engagement was militarily decisive in a limited sense — it opened the road to further Palestinian territory — but its significance was primarily doctrinal.

The Arab commanders understood their campaign not as territorial expansion in the conventional sense but as the execution of a religious mandate. The Quran's instruction in 9:29 — to fight the People of the Book until they pay the jizya in a state of subjugation — provided the explicit legal framework within which the campaigns were conducted. The Byzantine Empire, as the dominant Christian political power, was not merely a military opponent. It was, in the doctrinal framework of the Islamic state, the representative of a religious order that Islam was mandated to subordinate.

This framing matters enormously for understanding what followed. The Arab conquest of the Levant was not driven primarily by resource scarcity, demographic pressure, or conventional imperial ambition — though these factors were present. It was driven by a theological conviction that the expansion of Dar al-Islam at the expense of Dar al-Harb was a religious obligation. Raymond Ibrahim documents this in detail, noting that the early Arab commanders consistently framed their military objectives in explicitly theological terms, offering conquered populations the three classical choices: conversion, dhimmi subjugation, or death.

The Battle of Yarmouk, 636 AD — The Fall of Christian Syria

The Battle of Yarmouk, fought in August 636 along the Yarmouk River in present-day Jordan, is among the most consequential engagements in the history of Western civilization — and among the most consistently underweighted in contemporary historical education. A Byzantine army estimated at between 15,000 and 40,000 men, under the command of Emperor Heraclius's brother Theodore, was decisively defeated by an Arab force under Khalid ibn al-Walid over the course of six days of sustained fighting.

The consequences were civilizationally catastrophic for Christianity. Syria — the cradle of early Christian monasticism, home to Antioch where believers were first called Christians (Acts 11:26), site of the conversion of Paul, and the intellectual heartland of Eastern patristic theology — passed permanently from Christian political control. Damascus fell within weeks. The defeat at Yarmouk was not a temporary setback from which the Byzantine Empire would recover. It was the permanent amputation of Christianity's most historically rooted territory.

The theological dimension of this loss cannot be overstated. The Christian communities of Syria did not disappear immediately. They were subjected to the dhimmi system documented in the Pact of Umar: prohibited from building new churches, restricted in public religious expression, and burdened with the jizya tax that marked their institutional subordination. Over the following centuries, under sustained administrative and social pressure — not primarily through forced conversion but through the accumulated weight of second-class legal status — the Christian majority of Syria became a minority, and then a remnant. The demographic transformation that began at Yarmouk was completed not by the sword alone but by the grinding institutional logic of a system designed to make Christianity untenable as a public, civic identity.

This is the first demonstration of the pattern that would repeat across fourteen centuries: military defeat followed by institutional subordination followed by demographic erasure. The sword opened the door. The dhimmi system closed it behind Christianity permanently.

The Fall of Alexandria and Christian Egypt, 641 AD

Egypt fell to the Arab armies under Amr ibn al-As between 639 and 641 AD. The significance of this conquest for Christian civilization is almost impossible to overstate. Egypt was not a peripheral Christian territory. It was the intellectual capital of the ancient Christian world. Alexandria was the home of Origen, Clement, Athanasius — the figures whose theological work had shaped the Nicene Creed and defined the doctrinal boundaries of orthodox Christianity. The Catechetical School of Alexandria was the most sophisticated center of Christian learning in the ancient world. The monastic tradition that would later transform European Christianity had been born in the Egyptian desert under Anthony and Pachomius.

All of this was, within a generation of the Arab conquest, placed under Islamic administrative authority operating according to the same dhimmi framework applied in Syria. The Coptic Christian population — direct heirs of the theological tradition that had defined Christianity's intellectual identity — became a subjugated minority in their own historical homeland. They have remained so ever since: a community that has survived fourteen centuries of systematic institutional pressure by precisely the kind of spiritual militancy examined in Article 2 of this series, but that has never recovered the demographic or political standing it held before 641 AD.

The fall of Egypt also illustrates the civilizational stakes of military incompetence and internal division. The Byzantine Empire's failure to defend Egypt was compounded by the Miaphysite theological controversy: Egyptian Christians, persecuted by Constantinople for their doctrinal position on the nature of Christ, did not universally resist the Arab invasion with the commitment they might otherwise have shown. The theological divisions that Article 1 documented as internal weaknesses of the Christian world — the sectarian animosities Roosevelt identified — had direct military and civilizational consequences. A Christianity divided against itself at the doctrinal level was a Christianity strategically vulnerable at the military level.

The Fall of Carthage and the Erasure of North African Christianity, 698 AD

The Arab conquest of North Africa was completed with the fall of Carthage in 698 AD, following decades of intermittent campaign and resistance. The significance of this conquest is staggering in retrospect. North Africa was the homeland of Tertullian, the first major theologian to write in Latin and the originator of the concept of the Trinity as a theological formula. It was the homeland of Cyprian of Carthage, martyr and bishop, whose ecclesiology shaped Western Christianity's understanding of the Church. Most consequentially, it was the homeland of Augustine of Hippo — arguably the single most influential theologian in the history of Western Christianity, whose works on grace, free will, original sin, and political theology formed the intellectual foundation of both Catholic and Protestant traditions for over a millennium.

By 700 AD, North African Christianity — the tradition that had produced these figures — was administratively under Islamic control. By 1000 AD, it had been demographically eliminated. Unlike Egypt, where the Coptic community survived as a recognizable minority, North African Christianity vanished almost completely. The reasons are historically debated, but the structural dynamic is consistent with the pattern: institutional subordination through the dhimmi system, compounded in North Africa's case by the disruption of the episcopal and monastic structures that had sustained Christian community life, produced a demographic collapse from which Christianity in North Africa never recovered.

The theological irony is profound. Augustine's City of God — written partly in response to the sack of Rome in 410 AD — argued that the eternal city of God would endure regardless of the fate of earthly political structures. Within three centuries of his death, the earthly Christian community he had served and led ceased to exist in any meaningful form in his own homeland. The incompatibility between the Islamic political order and the survival of Christianity as a living demographic and institutional presence had produced, in North Africa, its most total historical demonstration.

III. The Western Front: Tours and the Salvation of European Christianity (732 AD)

The Arab advance into Western Europe had begun with the crossing of the Strait of Gibraltar in 711 AD under Tariq ibn Ziyad and the rapid conquest of the Visigothic Christian kingdom of Spain. Within seven years, Islamic forces had overrun virtually the entire Iberian Peninsula — a Christian civilization that had existed for three centuries — and had crossed the Pyrenees into Frankish territory. The speed of the conquest was a direct consequence of the military principles established in Article 2: Christian communities that could not or did not resist were absorbed into the dhimmi system; those that resisted insufficiently were overwhelmed.

By 732, Arab and Berber forces under Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi had advanced as far north as the Loire Valley, sacking the Basilica of Saint Hilary at Poitiers — one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Western Christendom — and threatening Tours, where the shrine of Saint Martin of Tours made it the spiritual center of Frankish Christianity. It was at this point that Charles Martel, Mayor of the Palace of the Frankish kingdom, intercepted the Islamic army.

The Battle of Tours — or Poitiers, as it is alternatively known — was fought in October 732. The engagement lasted approximately seven days. Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi was killed in the fighting, and the Islamic army withdrew southward without resuming its advance. Edward Gibbon's assessment, written in the eighteenth century, remains historically defensible: that a Muslim victory at Tours would have opened the road to the Rhine, and that without the Frankish military resistance represented by Charles Martel, the Islamization of Western Europe — with all its consequences for the Christian civilization that would subsequently produce the universities, the legal traditions, and the cultural frameworks of the modern West — was a genuine historical possibility.

The theological dimension of Tours is as significant as the military one. Charles Martel was not a saint. His methods for funding his military campaigns included the controversial seizure of Church properties, which earned him ecclesiastical censure. But the Church Militant tradition examined in Article 2 — the Augustinian framework of the just war as a legitimate expression of Christian love under conditions of threat — is precisely what Tours represents in practice. A Christian community that was willing and able to defend itself militarily survived. The Christian communities of Syria, Egypt, and North Africa, which had either been unable or insufficiently willing to do so, had not.

The consequences of Tours for Christian civilization were incalculable. The Frankish kingdom that Charles Martel defended became, under his grandson Charlemagne, the political foundation of the Holy Roman Empire and the institutional framework within which Western Christianity developed its distinctive theological, legal, and cultural character. The monasteries, the scholastic tradition, the canon law, the cathedral schools that would evolve into the first universities — all of this was built on the political stability that Tours made possible. It is not rhetorical excess to say that the Battle of Tours was one of the decisive civilizational engagements in human history.

IV. The Death of Eastern Christendom's Capital: The Fall of Constantinople, 1453 AD

The fall of Constantinople on May 29, 1453 to the Ottoman forces of Sultan Mehmed II is, by any measure, one of the most significant events in the history of Western civilization. It ended the Byzantine Empire — the continuous political successor to the Roman Empire of Constantine, the empire that had been the institutional guardian of Eastern Orthodox Christianity for over a thousand years. It transformed the greatest Christian cathedral in the world, the Hagia Sophia, into a mosque. And it sent shockwaves through the entirety of Christian Europe that fundamentally reoriented the geopolitical and psychological self-understanding of Western Christendom.

The siege had been anticipated for decades. Constantinople had survived repeated Islamic sieges since the seventh century — the Arab sieges of 674–678 and 717–718 AD had both failed, largely due to the Byzantine naval weapon known as Greek fire. The city's Theodosian Walls, constructed in the fifth century and never successfully breached by direct assault, had made it seemingly impregnable. Mehmed II's solution was technological: the deployment of massive Hungarian-cast cannon — the largest artillery pieces in the world at that time — to pound the walls into a state of structural failure over a seven-week bombardment. The final assault on May 29 breached the walls at multiple points simultaneously.

The last Byzantine Emperor, Constantine XI Palaiologos, died in the fighting — reportedly throwing off his imperial regalia and charging into the breach with his soldiers when the walls fell. His death was, in symbolic terms, the death of the Roman Empire itself: the unbroken political tradition stretching from Augustus through Constantine to the Christian emperors of Byzantium ended in the rubble of the Theodosian Walls.

The theological consequences were immediate and profound. The Hagia Sophia — built by Justinian in the sixth century as the supreme architectural expression of Eastern Christian civilization, the church in which the liturgy of the Eastern Orthodox tradition had been celebrated for nearly a thousand years — was converted into a mosque within days of the conquest. The minarets added to its exterior remain visible today. Its conversion was not merely symbolic vandalism. It was the physical enactment of the Quranic principle established in Article 1: that the territory of the Islamic state was the territory of God's governance, and that the visible signs of superseded religious traditions should be subordinated to the symbols of the superior revelation.

The fall of Constantinople had civilizational consequences that extended far beyond the immediate loss of the city. The flight of Byzantine scholars to Western Europe — carrying manuscripts of Greek classical and patristic literature that had been preserved in Constantinople's libraries — contributed directly to the acceleration of the Renaissance. The intellectual inheritance of classical Christian civilization was transmitted to the West precisely because Islamic conquest made it impossible to preserve it in its original home. There is a profound and painful irony in the fact that the Renaissance — the cultural movement that eventually produced the intellectual framework of modern Western civilization — was partly fertilized by the catastrophe of 1453.

The Greek Orthodox Christian community of Constantinople did not disappear immediately. Like the Coptic Christians of Egypt and the Syriac Christians of the Levant, they were subjected to the dhimmi system under Ottoman administration. The Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople survived as an institution — but in a condition of permanent institutional subordination that persists in modified form to the present day. The city that had been the capital of Eastern Christendom for over a thousand years is today Istanbul: a city in which the Christian community constitutes a fraction of one percent of the population.

The fall of Constantinople is the clearest single demonstration of the pattern at civilizational scale. A Christian capital, defended for over seven centuries against Islamic military pressure, fell when its defenses were finally overcome. What followed was not coexistence. It was the systematic transformation of a Christian civilization into an Islamic one — conducted not through mass execution but through the institutional logic of a system that made Christian public life progressively untenable over generations.

V. The Naval Dimension: The Battle of Lepanto, 1571 AD

By the mid-sixteenth century, Ottoman naval power had made the Mediterranean — historically the central highway of Christian civilization — effectively an Ottoman lake. The fall of Constantinople had been followed by the Ottoman conquest of Greece (completed by 1460), Serbia (1459), Bosnia (1463), and the steady advance into Central Europe that would culminate in the first siege of Vienna in 1529. Ottoman fleets raided the coastlines of Italy, Spain, and France with impunity, capturing hundreds of thousands of Europeans for the Ottoman slave markets — a practice directly grounded in the Quranic provisions for the treatment of kuffar documented in Article 1.

The immediate catalyst for the Battle of Lepanto was the Ottoman invasion and conquest of Cyprus in 1570–1571, during which the Venetian commander Marcantonio Bragadin was captured, subjected to prolonged torture, and publicly flayed alive by the Ottoman commander Lala Mustafa Pasha — an act that shocked Christian Europe and galvanized the formation of the Holy League under the papacy of Pius V.

The Holy League fleet — an alliance of Spain, Venice, the Papal States, Genoa, and several smaller Italian states — engaged the Ottoman fleet at the Gulf of Patras on October 7, 1571. The engagement was the largest naval battle in the Mediterranean since antiquity: approximately 400 galleys and 150,000 men on both sides. The Holy League victory was decisive. The Ottoman fleet lost approximately 200 vessels and 30,000 men killed or captured. An estimated 12,000 Christian galley slaves were liberated from Ottoman oars. It was the most complete naval defeat the Ottoman Empire had suffered in a century of Mediterranean dominance.

The theological framing of Lepanto by both sides is instructive. Pope Pius V had organized the Holy League explicitly as a crusading alliance, calling for the Rosary to be prayed across Christendom for the fleet's success. He subsequently attributed the victory to the intercession of the Virgin Mary and established the feast of Our Lady of Victory — later renamed Our Lady of the Rosary — in commemoration of the battle. On the Ottoman side, the engagement was understood as a campaign of Islamic expansion against Dar al-Harb, conducted under the same theological mandate that had driven the Arab conquests of the seventh century.

The consequences of Lepanto were significant but not as decisive as Tours or Vienna. The Ottoman Empire rebuilt its fleet within a year and retained its territorial gains in Cyprus. What Lepanto demonstrated was the limit of Ottoman naval capacity — the point beyond which the Islamic advance into Western Mediterranean waters could not be sustained against a unified Christian response. It broke the psychological aura of Ottoman invincibility at sea and preserved the maritime access that allowed Western European civilization to develop its Atlantic-facing orientation — the orientation that would ultimately produce the age of exploration and the global expansion of European power.

For the purposes of this article's central argument, Lepanto is significant as evidence of the continuous, uninterrupted nature of the Islamic-Christian military encounter. From Ajnadayn in 634 to Lepanto in 1571 — nearly a thousand years — the pattern had not changed. The theological mandate articulated in Quran 9:29 had not been superseded, reinterpreted into irrelevance, or replaced by a framework of peaceful coexistence. It had been enacted, with consistent institutional logic, across every century and every theater in which Islamic and Christian political power had come into contact.

VI. The Final Wave: The Siege of Vienna, 1683 AD

The Siege of Vienna in 1683 represents the high-water mark of Ottoman military advance into Central Europe and the definitive turning point after which Islamic military power would retreat rather than advance against European Christendom. It is also, in the arc of this article's argument, the final major demonstration of the principle that the survival of Christianity in Europe was contingent on the willingness of Christian powers to defend it by force of arms.

The Ottoman Grand Vizier Kara Mustafa Pasha led a force estimated at between 90,000 and 150,000 men to the walls of Vienna in July 1683, subjecting the city to a siege that lasted from July 14 to September 12. The city's garrison of approximately 15,000 men, under the command of Count Ernst Rüdiger von Starhemberg, held out for two months against a force that outnumbered them by a factor of six to one or more. The city was on the verge of falling when the relief force arrived.

The relief of Vienna was achieved by the largest cavalry charge in recorded history. King John III Sobieski of Poland led approximately 70,000 men — Polish, Austrian, and German forces united under the banner of the Holy League — in a charge down the slopes of the Kahlenberg hill into the Ottoman encampment on the afternoon of September 12. The Ottoman army broke and fled. Kara Mustafa Pasha was subsequently executed by strangulation on the orders of Sultan Mehmed IV — the Ottoman institutional response to the failure of a campaign that had been understood, on both sides, as a clash of civilizational systems.

Sobieski's framing of the engagement was explicitly theological. His dispatch to Pope Innocent XI after the battle adapted Caesar's report of his Gallic campaign: Venimus, vidimus, Deus vicit — "We came, we saw, God conquered." The battle was understood by Christian Europe not as a geopolitical victory but as the providential preservation of Christendom — the intervention of divine protection expressed through the willingness of Christian soldiers to place themselves between the Islamic advance and the heart of European civilization.

The consequences of 1683 were definitive at the strategic level. The Ottoman retreat from Vienna was followed by the Treaty of Karlowitz in 1699, under which the Ottoman Empire ceded Hungary, Transylvania, and large portions of the Balkans to the Habsburg Empire — the first major territorial reversal in Islamic expansion against European Christendom in nearly a thousand years. The demographic and institutional consequences of a successful Ottoman conquest of Vienna — and the subsequent Islamization of Central Europe through the dhimmi mechanism that had operated in Syria, Egypt, North Africa, and Anatolia — were averted at the cost of the lives of the men who fought and died on the slopes of the Kahlenberg.

The thousand-year arc from Yarmouk to Vienna thus yields a single, consistent historical conclusion: wherever Islamic military power was sufficient to overcome Christian resistance, Christianity disappeared as a living demographic and institutional presence over the course of subsequent generations. Wherever Christian military power was sufficient to repel or reverse the Islamic advance, Christianity survived. The correlation is not incidental. It reflects the structural logic of a system — documented in Article 1 — that was designed precisely to produce this outcome.

VII. The Root of the Incompatibility: Why the Conflict Was Never About Politics Alone

A secular historical reading of the conflicts documented above might locate their cause in geopolitics, resource competition, or the dynamics of imperial expansion. These factors were present. But a reading that reduces the Islamic-Christian encounter to secular political causes systematically misrepresents what both parties said about themselves and what the documentary record confirms they believed they were doing.

The Arab commanders who crossed into Byzantine Palestine in 634 were not primarily motivated by the need for agricultural land, though the Levant's fertility was strategically significant. They were motivated by a theological conviction — documented in their own recorded statements and grounded in the Quranic texts examined in Article 1 — that the expansion of Islamic governance over non-Muslim populations was a religious obligation. The classification of the world into Dar al-Islam (the House of Submission) and Dar al-Harb (the House of War) is not a metaphor. It is a legal category that defined the permanent condition of the relationship between Islamic political authority and non-Muslim populations until the latter were brought under Islamic governance.

The structural incompatibility is located precisely here. Christianity, in its mainstream theological tradition from Augustine through Aquinas and beyond, does not mandate the political subjugation of non-Christians. The Great Commission (Matthew 28:19–20) commands evangelization — the proclamation of the gospel — but not the imposition of Christian law on non-Christian populations by force of arms as a religious obligation. The distinction between spiritual authority and temporal political power — the "render unto Caesar" framework of Matthew 22:21 — created conceptual space for pluralistic political arrangements that classical Islamic jurisprudence, with its fusion of religious and political authority in the concept of the caliphate, did not recognize as legitimate.

Islam, as classically formulated, recognizes no legitimate separation between religious and political authority. The Ummah — the community of believers — is simultaneously a religious and a political entity. Its governance is not merely informed by divine revelation; it is constituted by it. Non-Muslim populations living under Islamic governance are not citizens in a pluralistic political framework. They are dhimmis — tolerated subjects whose civic status is defined by their religious inferiority and whose continued presence is conditional on their payment of the jizya and their acceptance of the restrictions that classical Islamic law imposes upon them.

These two systems — one that conceptually separates religious and political authority and one that fuses them — cannot coexist on equal terms within a shared political space. One must ultimately subordinate the other. The fourteen centuries of military and civilizational conflict documented in this article are the historical record of what happens when these two systems encounter each other with military force as the arbiter of their relationship.

The incompatibility is not, it bears repeating, a matter of individual Muslims being incapable of peaceful coexistence with individual Christians. Millions of Muslims and Christians have lived as neighbors, colleagues, and friends across history and across the world today. The incompatibility is structural and institutional: between the classical Islamic political theology of the caliphate and its legal instruments, and the existence of Christianity as a free, publicly expressed, demographically significant civilization. Wherever the former has had complete institutional authority, the latter has not survived.

VIII. The Unending Dimension: The Conflict That Has Not Concluded

The Treaty of Karlowitz in 1699 and the subsequent decline of Ottoman military power created a period in which the Islamic-Christian civilizational conflict appeared, to Western eyes, to have been resolved by the permanent ascendancy of European power. The colonial period reinforced this impression: European powers imposed political control over Muslim-majority territories from West Africa to Southeast Asia, and the Islamic world appeared to have been definitively subordinated to Western political and military dominance.

This impression was always historically shallow. The theological mandate documented in Quran 9:5 and 9:29 was not repealed by military defeat. The concept of Dar al-Harb was not dissolved by the colonial period. The classical Islamic jurisprudential tradition that defined the relationship between the Islamic state and non-Muslim populations did not undergo the kind of systematic theological reformation that would have been required to render it compatible with the pluralistic political arrangements of liberal democracy. What changed was the balance of military and political power, not the doctrinal framework that had produced the conflict.

The twentieth and twenty-first centuries have made this visible with renewed clarity. The collapse of the Ottoman caliphate in 1924 produced not a stable accommodation between Islam and Western political modernity, but a century of sustained contest over whether Islamic political theology — in its various modern reformulations, from the Muslim Brotherhood to Salafism to the Islamic State — would reassert itself as the governing framework of Muslim-majority societies. The pattern of that contest is recognizable from the historical record examined in this article: wherever Islamic political movements have achieved sufficient power to impose their framework on religious minorities, the structural dynamic of the dhimmi system has re-emerged in modified institutional forms.

The Christian populations of Iraq, Syria, Egypt, and Nigeria — to cite only the most documented contemporary cases — have experienced in the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries a demographic collapse that mirrors, in accelerated form, the pattern documented for Syria in the seventh century and North Africa in the eighth. Iraq's Christian population, estimated at approximately 1.5 million in 2003, had fallen below 300,000 by 2015 following the rise of the Islamic State and the preceding decades of targeted violence. Egypt's Coptic community — the direct demographic heir of the Christian civilization that produced Origen and Athanasius — faces sustained institutional discrimination, periodic mass violence, and a slow demographic attrition that continues in the present.

The conflict is not concluded. It has not been superseded by a new framework of genuine theological pluralism within the Islamic tradition. It continues according to the same structural logic that it has exhibited for fourteen centuries: the logic of a theological system that does not recognize the permanent legitimacy of Christianity as a co-equal civilization, and that consistently produces institutional pressure toward Christian demographic decline wherever it achieves political dominance.

To acknowledge this is not to call for hostility toward Muslim individuals or Muslim communities. It is to insist on historical honesty about what the record shows — and on the corresponding conclusion that the preservation of Christianity as a living civilization requires the same kind of clear-eyed understanding of the threat it faces that Charles Martel demonstrated at Tours, that Constantine XI demonstrated at the walls of Constantinople, and that John III Sobieski demonstrated on the slopes of the Kahlenberg.

The Church Militant — examined in Article 2 — is not a medieval relic. It is the historically demonstrated condition of Christianity's survival in a world that contains a political theology structurally committed to its subordination. The alternative to understanding this is not peaceful coexistence. It is the fate of the Christians of North Africa, of Anatolia, of Mesopotamia: communities that did not survive the encounter because they were not prepared for what the encounter actually was.

IX. Madagascar: A Christian Nation in a World Where the Conflict Continues

Madagascar presents itself, constitutionally, as a secular state. Its constitution guarantees freedom of religion and prohibits the establishment of a state religion. In practice, Madagascar is a predominantly Christian nation: between 80 and 85 percent of the population identifies as Christian, and the influence of Christian — primarily Protestant and Catholic — institutions on Malagasy public life, education, and civic culture is pervasive and historically deep-rooted, as examined in Article 2.

This combination — constitutional secularism with de facto Christian civilizational identity — is not unusual in the contemporary world. But it carries implications that the historical record examined in this article makes impossible to ignore. A civilization that is Christian in its cultural formation but secular in its institutional self-understanding is a civilization that has partly disarmed itself theologically. It retains the cultural inheritance of Christianity without the theological framework — the Church Militant tradition — that historically produced and defended that inheritance. It is, in this sense, living on capital it is not actively reproducing.

Madagascar's Muslim minority — concentrated in the northwest coastal regions and among Indo-Pakistani and Comorian trading communities — is not, at present, a political force seeking the kind of institutional transformation that would threaten the country's Christian civilizational character. The immediate threat to Malagasy Christianity is not Islamic military pressure. It is the same threat that faces Christianity across the contemporary world: theological illiteracy, institutional compromise, and the gradual erosion of the conviction that the faith is worth defending at any cost.

But the historical record demands that Malagasy Christians understand the broader civilizational context within which they exist. The conversation that began at Ajnadayn in 634 and continued through Yarmouk, Alexandria, Carthage, Tours, Constantinople, Lepanto, and Vienna has not ended. It continues today in Iraq, in Egypt, in Nigeria, in the Sahel — and it will eventually reach every corner of the world in which Christianity and political Islam exist in proximity, because the structural logic of the theological system that drives it has not changed.

Madagascar's Christian identity was forged, as Article 2 documented, in persecution. The martyrs of Ambohipotsy chose death rather than apostasy under Ranavalona I. That choice was not an accident of culture or personality. It was the expression of a conviction — that the faith was worth more than survival without it — that the Church Militant tradition has produced in every century and every context in which Christianity has been genuinely threatened. That same conviction is what preserved Christianity at Tours, at Lepanto, at Vienna.

The question for Malagasy Christians — as for Christians everywhere in the twenty-first century — is whether that conviction is still alive. Whether the faith received as inheritance from the martyrs of Ambohipotsy and the defenders of Vienna and the Coptic monks of Egypt is understood as something worth defending with the same clarity and courage that its preservation has historically required.

History does not offer a comfortable answer to that question. It offers only this: the civilizations that understood what they were defending, and were willing to defend it, survived. Those that did not, did not. The choice is not between conflict and peace. The choice is between clarity and illusion. And illusion, in this particular civilizational encounter, has a documented historical price.

NAHOANA NO TSY MIFANARAKA AMIN'NY FOTOTRA NY ISLAM SY NY KRISTIANISMA: EFATRA AMBIN'NY FOLO TAONJATO FIFANDONANA ARA-SIVILIZASIONA

Ity lahatsoratra ity no fahatelo amin'ny andian-dahatsoratra. Ny voalohany dia nanaraka ny votoatin'ny Sharia amin'ny maha-rafitra ara-dalàna sy ara-politika feno. Ny faharoa dia nandinika ny filàna ara-teolojia sy ara-tantara ny Ecclesia Militans — ny fahavononan'ny Kristianisma hiaro ny tenany amin'ny sehatra ara-panahy sy ara-batana. Ity lahatsoratra fahatelo ity dia mampiray ny roa ho amin'ny tohan-kevitra foibe: ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina azy ireo dia tsy voajanahary, ara-kolontsaina, na ara-politika. Izy io dia rafitra, ara-foto-pampianarana, ary maharitra — ary efatra ambin'ny folo taonjato ny tantara voarakitra no porofon'izany.

ⓘ Fanamarihana momba ny loharano voalohany: Ny angona ara-miaramila sy ara-tantara dia avy voalohany indrindra amin'i Raymond Ibrahim, Sword and Scimitar: Fourteen Centuries of War between Islam and the West (Da Capo Press, 2018). Ny fandalinana ara-teolojia dia miorina amin'ny loharano voalohany avy amin'ny Korany sy ny Testamenta Vaovao izay natsangana tao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 1 sy 2 amin'ny andiany ity. Ny loharano hafa ara-tantara dia voasoratra ao amin'ny fizarana fanamarihana.

I. Ny Tohan-kevitra: Ny Tsy Fitoviana Ara-toe-tsaina Dia Tsy Fiampangana — Famaritana Rafitra Izy

Ny filazana fa ny Islam sy ny Kristianisma dia tsy mifanaraka amin'ny fototra dia, ao amin'ny adihevitra ankehitriny, matetika heverina ho mampangorohoro. Hesorina ho vokatra ny tsy fahalalana, ny fanovana ara-politika, na ny fialonana tandrefana sisa tavela. Ity fanaovan-tsinontsinona ity dia endrika iray amin'ny tsi-fahalalana ny tantara — izay tsy afaka maharitra raha tsy amin'ny alalan'ny fandavana ny fandinihana ny tena lazain'ireo fomban-drazana roa tonta momba ny tenany, momba ny hafa, ary momba ny fomban'ny fahefana ara-politika sy ara-panahy ara-dalàna.

Ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina dia tsy raharaha tsy fitoviana ara-kolontsaina. Ny kolontsaina dia afaka miara-miaina, mifangaro, ary mifanakaloza. Tsy raharaha fifaninana ara-politika izy io. Ny fifaninana ara-politika dia afaka manao fifanarahana sy mizara sisin-tany amin'ny fampandriampahalemana. Ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina dia ara-fotopampianarana: ny Islam sy ny Kristianisma dia manao fitakiana manerana izao tontolo izao, manokana, sy feno momba ny natioran'Andriamanitra, ny votoatin'ny fanambarana, ny famahana ara-nety ny fiarahamonina olombelona, ary ny anjaran'ny zanak'olombelona. Ireny fitakiana ireny dia tsy samy hafa fotsiny. Eo am-pototry ny zava-drehetra, mifanohitra izy ireo.

Ny Kristianisma dia mampianatra fa i Jesus Christ no Zanak'Andriamanitra niseho tamin'ny nofo, fa ny fahafatesana sy ny fitsanganan'ny tenany tamin'ny maty no asa farany amin'ny fanavotana ny olombelona, ary fa ny famonjena dia azo amin'ny finoana Azy. Ny Islam dia mandà mazava tsara sy miorina amin'ny fototra ireo fitakiana rehetra ireo. Ny Korany dia milaza fa Andriamanitra tsy manana zanaka (Q. 19:35), fa ny fijaliany tsy nitranga araka ny fahatakarana ny Kristianina azy (Q. 4:157), ary fa izay manome mpiara-miasa amin'Andriamanitra — izay ampiharin'ny fitsipika Islamika klasika amin'ny Kristianisma Triniterana — dia manao ny iray amin'ireo ota tsy azo ambadikin'Andriamanitra ny shirk (Q. 4:48).

Ireny dia tsy fifandirana am-pisipisika. Mifandraika amin'ny maha-izy an'Andriamanitra, ny natioran'ny famonjena, ary ny maha-marina ny lovam-pinoana teolojia kristiana manontolo. Ny Islam dia tsy mifanohitra amin'ny Kristianisma fotsiny. Milaza mazava tsara fa ny Kristianisma, amin'ny endrika ara-dalàna azy, dia fielezan'ny finoana nandravina avy amin'ny fanambarana Monotheista voalohany — iray izay tsy maintsy handoa, amin'ny faritra rehetra eo ambanin'ny fitantanana Islamika, ny fahefana ambony ny lalàna Korany.

Ny tantaran'ny fifanenana amin'ireo sivilizasiona roa ireo dia, amin'ity fahazavana ity, tsy tantaran'ny tsy fahatakarana. Tantaran'ny rafitra roa miasa araka ny filaharany anatiny ihany izy io — ary ny antontan'asa amin'io fifanenana io, nandritra ny telo ambin'ny folo taonjato sy ny kaontinanta telo, no tadiavin'ity lahatsoratra ity.

II. Ny Fifandrantana Voalohany: Ny Empire Byzantin sy ny Fandresen'ny Arabo (634–641 AD)

The Battle of Ajnadayn, 634 AD

Tao anatin'ny roa taona taorian'ny fahafatesan'ny Mpaminany Muhammad tamin'ny 632 AD, ny tafikan' ny fanjakana Islamika vao teraka dia efa niampita ho ao amin'ny Palestine Byzantine. Ny fifanandrinana lehibe voalohany teo amin'ny tafika Silamo sy Kristiana dia nitranga tao Ajnadayn tamin'ny Jolay 634, izay nandresy ny tafika Arabo notarihin'i Khalid ibn al-Walid ny tafika Byzantine niaro ny sisin-tany atsimo ny Levant. Ny fifanandrinana dia manapaka ara-miaramila amin'ny lafiny voafetra — nanokatra ny lalana ho any amin'ny faritra Palestine hafa — saingy ny dikany dia ara-foto-pampianarana voalohany indrindra.

Ny mpitari-tafika Arabo dia nahatakatra ny fampahanany tsy ho fanitarana ny faritany amin'ny fomba mahazatra fa ho fanatanterahana baiko ara-pivavahana. Ny fanamarahan'ny Korany ao amin'ny 9:29 — miady amin'ny Ahl al-Kitab mandra-pandoany ny jizya amin'ny toe-javatra na fanoloran-tena — dia nanome ny rafitra ara-dalàna mazava izay nampandeha ny fampahanana. Ny Empire Byzantin, amin'ny maha-fahefana politika Kristiana manandratra azy, dia tsy mpanohitra ara-miaramila fotsiny. Izy io, ao amin'ny rafitra araky ny foto-pampianaran'ny fanjakana Islamika, dia solon-tenan'ny filaharan'ny ara-pivavahana izay nampanao ny Islam hijery azy.

Ity famolavolana ity dia manan-danja be ho an'ny fahatakarana izay nanaraka. Ny fandresen'ny Arabo ny Levant dia tsy nampandehanin'ny fahafahan-tsakafo, ny tsindry demografika, na ny fitiavam-pahefana mahazatra — na dia nisy aza ireo antony ireo. Nampandehanin'ny fianianana ara-teolojika ny fanitarana ny Dar al-Islam amin'ny vidiny ny Dar al-Harb no adidy ara-pivavahana. I Raymond Ibrahim dia manisy antontan'asa izany amin'ny antsipirihany, mamaritra fa ny mpitari-tafika Arabo tany am-boalohany dia matetika namaritra ny tanjon'izy ireo ara-miaramila amin'ny teny ara-teolojika mazava tsara, nanome ny mponina nobaboina ny safidy klasika telo: fiovana amin'ny finoana, fanoloran-tena dhimmi, na fahafatesana.

The Battle of Yarmouk, 636 AD — Ny Fahalavoan'ny Syria Kristianina

The Battle of Yarmouk, izay niseho tamin'ny Aogositra 636 teny amoron'ny Reniranon'i Yarmouk ao amin'ny Jordan ankehitriny, dia iray amin'ireo fifandonana lehibe indrindra teo amin’ny tantaran’ny sivilizasiona tandrefana — nefa matetika no hodian-tsy hita ao amin’ny fampianarana tantara amin’izao fotoana izao. Ny tafika Byzantine, izay tombanana ho teo anelanelan’ny 15.000 sy 40.000 teo ho eo sady notarihin’i Theodore (rahalahin’ny Emperora Heraclius), dia resy tanteraka teo anatrehan’ny tafika Arabo notarihin’i Khalid ibn al-Walid, rehefa nifampitana nandritra ny enina andro.

Ny vokany dia mahatsiravina ara-sivilizasiona ho an'ny Kristianisma. Syria — ilay fitoerana niforonan’ny Kristianisma tany am-boalohany, fonenan’i Antiokia izay niantsoana ny mpino ho Kristiana voalohany (Asa 11:26), toerana niovan’i Paoly ho kristiana, ary ivon’ny fampianarana teolojia tatsinanana — dia niala tanteraka teo am-pelatanan’ny fahefana Kristiana. Lavo tao anatin’ny herinandro monja i Damaskosy. Ny faharesena tao Yarmouk dia tsy fahaverezana vonjimaika fotsiny izay azon’ny Empira Bizantina nahitsy; fa fanapahana maharitra ny faritra niorim-paka indrindra tamin’ny tantaran’ny Kristianisma izany.

Tsy azo hamaivanina ny vokatry ny faharesena teo amin’ny lafiny teolojika. Tsy nanjavona teo no ho eo akory ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana tany Syria, fa napetraka teo ambany fifehezan'ny rafitra dhimmi araka ny "Pact of Umar": norarana ny fananganana fiangonana vaovao, noferana ny fanehoana ny finoana ampahibemaso, ary nampitondraina ny vesatry ny hetra Jizya ho mariky ny fankatoavana. Nandritra ny taonjato maro, noho ny tsindry ara-pitantanana sy ara-tsosialy — nefa tsy tamin’ny alalan’ny fanerena hanova finoana mivantana fa noho ny vokatry ny naha-olom-pirenena kilasy faharoa azy ireo — dia nanjary vitsy an’isa ny Kristiana tao Syria, ary taty aoriana dia sisa tavela kely sisa. Ny fiovana ara-demôgrafia nanomboka tao Yarmouk dia tsy tamin’ny sabatra fotsiny, fa tamin’ny alalan’ny lamina izay nahatonga ny Kristianisma tsy ho afaka hita maso intsony eo amin’ny fiainana iombonana.

Ity no fanehoana voalohany ny endrika izay hamerina mandritra ny efatra ambin'ny folo taonjato: faharesena ara-miaramila narahin'ny fanoloran-tena orina narahin'ny fandroahana demografika. Ny sabatra nanokatra ny varavarana. Ny rafitra dhimmi dia nakatona azy tao ambadiky ny Kristianisma maharitra.

Ny Fahalavoan'i Alexandria sy ny Egypta Kristianina, 641 AD

Ejipta dia lavo tamin'ny tafika Arabo notarihin'i Amr ibn al-As teo anelanelan'ny 639 sy 641 AD. Ny dikan'ity fandresen'ity ho an'ny sivilizasiona Kristiana dia saika tsy azo lazaina be loatra. Ejipta tsy faritra am-pisipisika Kristiana. Izy io no renivohitr'ara-tsaina ny tontolon'ny Kristiana fahiny. Alexandria no tranon'i Origen, Clement, Athanasius — ireo olona izay ny asany ara-teolojia dia namolavola ny Nicene Creed sy namaritana ny sisintany ara-fotopampianarana ny Kristianisma ara-dalàna. Ny Catechetical School of Alexandria dia ny ivon'ny fahalalana Kristiana izay miezaka indrindra tamin'izany tontolo izany. Ny lovam-pinoana monastika izay hamolavola ny Kristianisma eropeana taty aoriana dia teraka tany an-efitry Ejipta teo ambanin'i Anthony sy Pachomius.

Ireo rehetra ireo dia, tao anatin'ny taranaka iray taorian'ny fandresen'ny Arabo, napetraka eo ambanin'ny fahefana fitantanana Islamika miasa araka ny rafitra dhimmi mitovy nampiharina tao Syria. Ny mponina Kristiana Coptic — mpandova mivantana ny lovam-pinoana teolojia izay namaritana ny maha-izy ny sain'ny Kristianisma — lasa vitsy an'isa nanoloran-tena tany amin'ny tanindrazan'izy ireo ara-tantara. Efa naharitra toy izany hatramin'izay izy ireo: fiaraha-monina izay velona nandritra ny telo ambin'ny folo taonjato ny tsindry orina rafitra amin'ny karazany ny fijoroana ara-panahy voamarika ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 2 amin'ny andiany ity, saingy tsy nahatafaverina ny maha-demografika na ny toerana politika nitananany talohan'ny 641 AD.

Ny fahalavoan'ny Ejipta dia mampiseho koa ny herim-po ara-sivilizasiona ny tsy fahaizan'ny miaramila sy ny fizarana anatiny. Ny tsy fahaizan'ny Empire Byzantin hiaro an'i Egypta dia nampitomby ny fifandirana teolojika Miaphysite: ny Kristiana Egypsiana, noenjehina an'i Constantinople noho ny toerana ara-foto-pampianarana momba ny natioran'i Kristy, dia tsy nanohitra ny fanafihan'ny Arabo manerana ny rehetra tamin'ny fanokafana azony seho toy izany. Ny fizarana ara-teolojika voarakitra tao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 1 ho fahalemen'ny tontolo Kristiana anatiny — ny fifandirana ara-pivavahana nambaran'i Roosevelt — dia nananany vokany ara-miaramila sy ara-sivilizasiona mivantana. Ny Kristianisma nizara mafy teo amin'ny sehatra ara-poto-pampianarana dia Kristianisma malemy ara-stratejika teo amin'ny sehatra ara-miaramila.

Ny Nahalavoan'i Carthage sy ny Fandroahana ny Kristianisma tao Afrika Afovoany, 698 AD

Ny fandresen'ny Arabo ny Afrika Afovoany dia vita tamin'ny fahalavoan'i Carthage tamin'ny 698 AD, taorian'ny taona maro ny fampahanana tsy mitohy sy ny fanoherana. Ny dikany amin'ity fandresen'ity dia mahagaga rehefa dinihina. Ny Afovoany Afriky no tanindrazany Tertullian, ilay teolojiana lehibe voalohany nanoratra tamin'ny teny Latin ary inventor ny fomba mamaritra ny Trinity amin'ny lafiny teolojia. Izy io no tanindrazany Cyprian of Carthage, martira sy eveka, izay ny ekleziaziny dia namolavola ny fahatakarana ny Kristianisma Tandrefana ny Fiangonana. Ny manan-danja indrindra, izy io no tanindrazany Augustine of Hippo — mety ho teolojiana nisy fitaomana be indrindra amin'ny tantaran'ny Kristianisma Tandrefana, izay ny asany momba ny fahasoavana, ny sitrapo malalaka, ny ota tany am-boalohany, sy ny teolojia politika dia namolavola ny fototra ara-tsaina ny lovam-pinoana Katolika sy Protestanta mandritra ny arivo taona mahery.

Tamin'ny 700 AD, ny Kristianisma tao Afrika Afovoany — ny lovam-pinoana izay namokatra ireo olona ireo — dia eo ambanin'ny fifehezana Islamika ara-pitantanana. Tamin'ny 1000 AD, hadino ara-demografika izy io. Tsy toy ny Ejipta, izay velona eo anivon'ny fiaraha-monina Coptic ho vitsy an'isa hita, ny Kristianisma tao Afrika Afovoany dia nandao saika tanteraka. Ny antony dia adihevitra ara-tantara, saingy ny fihevitr'ny rafitra dia mifanaraka amin'ny endrika: fanoloran-tena orina amin'ny alalan'ny rafitra dhimmi, nampitomby tao Afrika Afovoany ny fisarahana ny rafitra episkopaly sy monastika izay nitehiry ny fiainan'ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana, dia namokatra fianjerana demografika tsy ahafahan'ny Kristianisma hiverina tany Afrika Afovoany.

Ny fetsy ara-teolojika dia lalina. Ny City of God nataon'i Augustin — nosoratana ampahany ho valiny amin'ny fandroboan'i Roma tamin'ny 410 AD — dia nanery fa ny tanàna mandrakizay an'Andriamanitra dia haharitra na inona na inona ny anjaran'ny rafitra politika an-tany. Tao anatin'ny taonjato telo taorian'ny nahafatesany, ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana an-tany izay nasainy sy notarihiny dia nitsahatra tsy misy intsony amin'ny endrika manan-danja tany amin'ny tanindrazany. Ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina eo amin'ny filaharan'ny politika Islamika sy ny fahaveloman'ny Kristianisma ho fanatrehan'ny demografika miaina sy orina dia namokatra, tany Afrika Afovoany, ny fanehoana ara-tantara feno indrindra.

III. Ny Sehatra Tandrefana: Tours sy ny Famonjena ny Kristianisma Eropeana (732 AD)

Ny fandrosoana Arabo ho any Eoropa Tandrefana dia nanomboka tamin'ny fiaingana ny Détroit de Gibraltar tamin'ny 711 AD teo ambanin'i Tariq ibn Ziyad sy ny fandresena haingana ny fanjakan'ny Kristiana Visigothic any Espaina. Tao anatin'ny fito taona, ny tafika Islamika dia efa saiky nandresy ny Iberian Peninsula manontolo — sivilizasiona Kristiana nisy nandritra ny telo taonjato — ary niampita ny Pyrenees ho any amin'ny faritry ny Fanjakana Franks. Ny hafainganan'ny fandresena dia vokatra mivantana ny fitsipika miaramila voatsangan'ny Lahatsoratra 2: ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana tsy afaka na tsy nanohitra dia voaray ao amin'ny rafitra dhimmi; ireo nanohitra tsy ampy dia roretsana.

Tamin'ny 732, ny tafika Arabo sy Berbery notarihin'i Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi dia efa nitaona lavitra avaratra ho any amin'ny Vallée de La Loire, nandrava ny Basilique de Saint Hilaire ao Poitiers — iray amin'ireo toerana fanompoana manan-danja indrindra tany Christendom Tandrefana — ary nandrahona an'i Tours, izay ivon'ny fiainana ara-panahin’ny Royaume Franc noho ny fisian’ny fiangonana masin’i Saint Martin de Tours. Teo amin’izay fotoana izay no niditra an-tsehatra i Charles Martel, ben'ny lapa (Mayor of the Palace) tao amin'ny fanjakan’ny Franka, mba hisakana ny fandrosoan'ny tafika Silamo/Islamika.

The Battle of Tours — na Poitiers, araka ny fiantsoana azy hafa — dia niady tamin'ny Oktobra 732. Ny fifanandrinana dia naharitra andro fito eo ho eo. I Abd al-Rahman al-Ghafiqi dia maty tamin'ny ady, ary ny tafika Islamika dia ningitrika tany atsimo tsy namerina ny fandrosoany. Ny fandinihin'i Edward Gibbon, nosoratana tamin'ny taonjato fahavalo ambin'ny folo, dia mbola azo arovan'ny ara-tantara: fa ny fandresena Silamo tao Tours dia hanokatra ny lalana ho any Fleuve du Rhin, ary raha tsy nisy ny fanoherana ara-miaramila Franque asehon'i Charles Martel, ny Islamisation an'i Eoropa Tandrefana — amin'ny vokany rehetra ho an'ny sivilizasiona Kristiana izay hiteraka avy eo ny oniversite, ny lovam-pinoana ara-dalàna, ary ny rafitra ara-kolontsainan'ny Andrefana maoderina — dia mety ho nitranga tokoa.

Tahaka ny lafiny ara-miaramila, dia manan-danja lehibe koa ny lafiny ara-teolojika tamin’ny ady tao Tours. Tsy olo-masina i Charles Martel; ny fomba namatsiany ny tafiny dia nahitana ny fakana ny fananan’ny Fiangonana, izay niteraka fanakianana mafy tamin'izany fotoana izany. Kanefa, ny lovam-pinoana Ecclesia Militans (ny Fiangonana Miady) — sy ny rafitry ny "ady marina" (bellum iustum) araka ny fampianaran'i Saint Augustin, izay milaza fa ny fiarovana ny tanindrazana dia fanehoana ny fitiavana Kristiana manoloana ny fanafihana — no tena nanjaka tao Tours. Ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana izay vonona sy nahay niaro tena ara-miaramila dia tafavoaka velona. Fa ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana tany Syria, Ejipta, ary Afrika Avaratra, izay tsy afaka na tsy vonona nanao izany, dia nanjavona na nampahantraina.

Ny vokany an'i Tours ho an'ny sivilizasiona Kristiana dia tsy azo tsaohana. Ny fanjakana Frankia niaro'i Charles Martel dia lasa, teo ambanin'ny zafy Charles Martel Charlemagne, ny fototra politika ny Holy Roman Empire sy ny rafitra orina izay niteraky ny Kristianisma Tandrefana ny toetrany ara-teolojia, ara-dalàna, ary ara-kolontsaina manokana. Ny monastera, ny lovam-pinoana skolastika, ny lalànan'ny Canon, ny sekoly katedraly izay hiteraka ny oniversite voalohany — ireo rehetra ireo dia naorina tamin'ny fampandriampahalemana politika nahatonga an'i Tours ho azo atao. Tsy fanitarana ara-teny ny milaza fa The Battle of Tours dia iray amin'ireo fifanandrinana ara-sivilizasiona manapaka amin'ny tantaran'ny zanak'olombelona.

IV. Ny Fahafatesan'ny Renivohitry ny Christendom Atsinanana: Ny Fahalavoan'i Konstantinopla, 1453 AD

Ny nahalavoan'i Constantinople ny 29 Mey 1453 tamin'ny tafika Ottoman an'i Sultan Mehmed II dia, na inona na inona ny fandanjana, iray amin'ireo zava-nitranga manan-danja indrindra amin'ny tantaran'ny sivilizasiona Tandrefana. Namarana ny Empire Byzantin izy io — ny mpandimby ara-politika maharitra ny Empire Romana an'i Constantine, ny fanjakana izay niaro orina ny Kristianisma Ortodoksa Atsinanana nandritra ny arivo taona mahery. Nanova ny katedrala Kristiana lehibe indrindra amin'izao tontolo izao, ny Hagia Sophia, ho mosqué. Ary nandefa fampangorohoro nanerana ny Eoropa Kristiana manontolo nanamboatra indray ny fahatakarana jeopolitika sy ara-tsaina ny tenany an'i Christendom Tandrefana.

Ny fahirano dia nandrasana nandritra ny taona maro. Constantinople dia naharitra ny fahirano Islamika maromaro hatramin'ny taonjato faha-7 — ny fahirano Arabo tamin'ny 674–678 sy 717–718 AD dia samy tsy nahomby, vokatry ny fitaovana naval Byzantine fantatra amin'ny anarana hoe Greek fire. Ny Theodosian Walls ny tanàna, naorina tamin'ny taonjato fahadimy ary tsy notabahina na oviana na oviana tamin'ny fanafihana mivantana, dia nanao azy ho toa tsy azo baboina. Ny vahaolana nentin'i Mehmed II dia ara-teknolojia: ny fampiasana baody lehibe natao tany Hungary — ny fitaovana fanafihana lehibe indrindra tamin'izany fotoana izany — mba hampikapy ny rindrina ho amin'ny fahalavoany rafitra nandritra ny fandoroana fito herinandro. Ny fanafihana farany ny 29 Mey dia nitabaka ny rindrina tamin'ny toerana maro niaraka.

Ny Emperora Byzantine farany, Constantine XI Palaiologos, dia maty tamin'ny ady — lazaina fa nalainy ny fitafiany imperial ary niditra tamin'ny tafika ny miaramila rehefa lavo ny rindrina. Ny fahafatesany dia, amin'ny fomba fanehoana, ny fahafatesan'ny Empire Romana mihitsy: ny lovam-pinoana politika tsy tapaka nanomboka tamin'i Augustus ka hatramin'i Constantine ho any amin'ny emperora Kristiana an'i Byzantium dia vita tamin'ny fasika ny Theodosian Walls.

Ny vokany ara-teolojika dia avy hatrany sy lalina. Ny Hagia Sophia — naorin'i Justinien tamin'ny taonjato faha-6 amin'ny maha-fanehoana ara-toeran-trano ambony indrindra ny sivilizasiona Kristiana Atsinanana, ny fiangonana izay nanaovana ny litorjia ny lovam-pinoana Ortodoksa Atsinanana nandritra ny arivo taona eo ho eo — dia novana ho mosqué tao anatin'ny andro vitsivitsy taorian'ny faharesena. Ny minareta nampiana tamin'ny seranany ivelany dia mbola hita ankehitriny. Ny fiovany dia tsy fandripahana famantarana fotsiny. Izy io no fanatanterahana ara-batana ny fitsipiky ny Korany voatsangan'ny Lahatsoratra 1: fa ny faritry ny fanjakana Islamika dia faritry ny fitantanan'Allah, ary ny famantarana hita maso ny lovam-pinoana ara-pivavahana nisolo tena dia tsy maintsy ampidinina ho amin'ny famantarana ny fanambarana ambony.

Ny nahalavoan'i Constantinople dia nanan-danja ara-sivilizasiona lavitra ny fahaverezan'ny tanàna avy hatrany. Ny fandosiran'ny manam-pahaizana Byzantins ho any Eoropa Tandrefana — nitondra soratra ny literatiora Grika klasika sy patristica izay voatahiry tao amin'ny tranonolaran'i Constantinople — dia nandray anjara mivantana tamin'ny fanentanana ny Renaissance. Ny lovan'ny sivilizasiona Kristiana klasika dia navoaka ho any Andrefana mivantana noho ny fandresen'ny Islamika nahatonga azy tsy azo tehirizina tao amin'ny tranony voalohany. Misy fetsy lalina sy marary amin'ny zava-misy fa ny Renaissance — ny hetsika ara-kolontsaina izay niteraka avy eo ny rafitra ara-tsaina ny sivilizasiona Tandrefana maoderina — dia ampahany novonoin'ny loza 1453.

Ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana Ortodoksa Grika an'i Constantinople dia tsy nandao niaraka tamin'izay. Toy ny Kristiana Coptic an'i Ejipta sy ny Kristiana Syriac ny Levant, izy ireo dia narohana amin'ny rafitra dhimmi eo ambanin'ny fitantanana Ottoman. Ny Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople dia velona amin'ny maha-orina — saingy amin'ny toe-javatra fanoloran-tena orina maharitra izay maharitra amin'ny endrika novaina hatramin'ny ankehitriny. Ny tanàna izay renivohitr'ny Christendom Atsinanana nandritra ny arivo taona mahery dia Istanbul ankehitriny: tanàna izay ny fiaraha-monina Kristiana dia ampahany iray amin'ny isanjaton'ny mponina.

Ny fahalavoan'i Constantinople no fanehoana tokana mazava indrindra ny endrika amin'ny skalanan'ny sivilizasiona. Renivohitra Kristiana, niaro nandritra ny taonjato fito mahery tamin'ny tsindry ara-miaramila Islamika, lavo rehefa roretsana tamin'ny farany ny fiarovana. Izay nanaraka dia tsy fiara-miaina. Izy io no fanovana rafitra ny sivilizasiona Kristiana ho Islamika — natao tsy amin'ny famonoana be fa amin'ny lojikan'ny orina rafitra izay nahatonga ny fiainan'ny ampahibemaso Kristiana ho tsy azo atao amin'ny herin-taona.

V. Ny Lafiny teny an-dranomasina: The Battle of Lepanto, 1571 AD

Tamin'ny tapaky ny taonjato faha-16, ny herin'ny force navale Ottoman dia efa nahatonga ny Méditerranée — ara-tantara ny lalana foibe ny sivilizasiona Kristiana — ho farihy Ottoman azo lazaina. Ny fahalavoan'i Constantinople dia narahin'ny fandresen'ny Ottoman ny Gresy (vita tamin'ny 1460), Serbia (1459), Bosnia (1463), ary ny fandrosoana mitohy ho any Eoropa Afovoany izay hatramin'ny fahirano voalohany an'i Vienna tamin'ny 1529. Ny filamin'ny Ottoman dia namely ny amorony ny Italia, Espaina, sy Frantsa tsy ampahibemaso, nitantana ny Eoropeanina an-tapitrisany ho any amin'ny tsenan'andevo Ottoman — fanaovana miorina mivantana amin'ny fepetra Koranika momba ny fitondrana ny kuffar voarakitra ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 1.

Ny antony avy hatrany ny The Battle of Lepanto dia ny fanafihan'ny Ottoman sy ny fandreseny an'i Cyprus tamin'ny 1570–1571, izay ny mpitari-tafika Venetianina Marco Antonio Bragadin dia voasambotra, narohana ny fanenjehana maharitra, ary nokatsohinany hoditry ny velona ampahibemaso araka ny didin’ny mpitari-tafika Ottoman Lala Mustafa Pasha — asa izay nampangorohoro ny Eoropa Kristiana sy nahazoana hery ny Holy League eo ambanin’ny Papacy an’i Pius V. Ny ady tao Lepanto tamin’ny 1571 dia niafara tamin’ny faharesen’ny Ottoman sy ny fandresen’ny Holy League.

Ny filamin'ny Holy League — firaisambe an'ny Spain, Venice, ny Papal States, Republic of Genoa, sy ny fanjakana Italia kely maro — dia niady tamin'ny filamin'ny Ottoman tao amin'ny Gulf of Patras ny 7 Oktobra 1571. Ny fifaninanana dia ny ady an-dranomasina lehibe indrindra tao Méditerranée hatramin'ny fahagola: 400 galleys eo ho eo sy lehilahy miisa 150,000 teo amin'ny lafiny roa. Ny fandresen'ny Holy League dia manapaka. Ny filamin'ny Ottoman dia very 200 sambo eo ho eo sy lehilahy 30,000 maty na voasambotra. Andevo galley Kristiana 12,000 eo ho eo no nafahana tamin'ny kibay Ottoman. Izy io no défaite navale feno indrindra ny Ottoman Empire nandritra ny taonjato iray ny fitondrany ny Méditerranée.

Ny famolavolana ara-teolojika an'i Lepanto avy amin'ny lafiny roa dia mampianatra. I Papa Pius V dia nikarakara ny Holy League ho firaisambe Croisade mazava tsara, niantso ny Rosary hoivohana nanerana ny Christendom ho an'ny fahombiazana ny filaminy. Avy eo nampihany ny fandresena ho amin'ny fivavahan'ny Virgin Mary ary nametraka ny fety Our Lady of Victory — nanova anarana avy eo ho Our Lady of the Rosary — ho fahatsiarovana ny ady. Teo amin'ny lafiny Ottoman, ny fifanandrinana dia noheverina ho fampahanana ny fanitarana Islamika nanoherana ny Dar al-Harb, natao eo ambanin'ny baiko ara-teolojika mitovy izay nampandeha ny fandresen'ny Arabo tamin'ny taonjato faha-7.

Ny vokan'i Lepanto dia lehibe saingy tsy manapaka toy ny Tours na Vienna. Ny Ottoman Empire dia namboatra indray ny filamin'ny tao anatin'ny taona iray ary nitehiry ny faritany nobaboiny tany Cyprus. Izay naneho an'i Lepanto dia ny fetrany ny herin'ny force navale Ottomane — ny teboka tsy azo notohizana ny fandrosoana Islamika tao amin'ny faritr'i Mediterranean Tandrefana nanoherana ny valiny Kristiana mitambatra. Nitabaka ny vondrom-pitaoman'ny tsy azo resena an'ny Ottoman tao amin'ny ranomasina izy io ary nitehiry ny fidirana maritime izay nahafahan'ny sivilizasiona Eoropa Tandrefana namolavola ny filaharany manodidina ny Atlantic — ny filaharany izay hiteraka avy eo ny vanim-potoana fandalovan'ny ranomasina sy ny fanitarana maneran-tany ny herin'ny Eoropa.

Ho an'ny tohan-kevitra foibe ity lahatsoratra ity, ny Lepanto dia manan-danja amin'ny maha-porofo ny natioran'ny fifanenana ara-miaramila Islamika-Kristiana mitohy tsy tapaka. Hatramin'i Ajnadayn tamin'ny 634 ka hatramin'i Lepanto tamin'ny 1571 — arivo taona eo ho eo — ny endrika dia tsy niova. Ny baiko ara-teolojika voasoratra ao amin'ny Quran 9:29 dia tsy nosoloana, nandikana ho tsy manan-danja, na nosoloana rafitra fiara-miaina am-pilaminana. Natao, amin'ny lojika orina mitohy, nandritra ny taonjato rehetra sy ny tantana rehetra izay nifanena amin'ny herin'ny politika Islamika sy Kristiana.

VI. Ny Onja Farany: The Siege of Vienna, 1683 AD

The Siege of Vienna tamin'ny 1683 dia maneho ny tampony ny fandrosoana ara-miaramila Ottoman ho any Eoropa Afovoany sy ny teboka mivadika farany izay hidiran'ny heriny ara-miaramila Islamika hiala fa tsy hisy hamonjy intsony ny Christendom Eropeana. Izy io koa, ao amin'ny filaharany ny tohan-kevitry ity lahatsoratra ity, ny fanehoana farany lehibe ny fitsipika fa ny fahaveloman'ny Kristianisma tany Eoropa dia miankina amin'ny fahavononan'ny fahefana Kristiana hiaro azy amin'ny herin'ny fitaovam-piadiana.

Ny Grand Vizier Ottoman Kara Mustafa Pasha dia nitarika hery noheverina ho eo anelanelan'ny lehilahy 90,000 sy 150,000 ho amin'ny rindrin'i Vienna tamin'ny Jolay 1683, nanao fahirano ny tanàna nanomboka ny 14 Jolay ka hatramin'ny 12 Septambra. Ny tafika mpiaro ny tanàna eo ho eo lehilahy 15,000, notarihin'i Count Ernst Rüdiger von Starhemberg, dia naharitra roa volana nanoherana hery nandresy azy tamin'ny anjara enin-kahanina na mahery kokoa. Ny tanàna dia efa saika ho lavo rehefa tonga ny tafika fanafoanana.

Ny fanafoanana an'i Vienna dia vita tamin'ny fanafihana kavaleria lehibe indrindra voarakitra amin'ny tantara. Ny Mpanjaka John III Sobieski avy any Poland dia nitarika lehilahy 70,000 eo ho eo — la force polognaise, Aostria, sy ny Holy Roman Empire mitambatra eo ambanin'ny sainam-pirenena Holy League — tamin'ny fanafihana midina ny hantsana Kahlenberg ho any amin'ny toby Ottoman ny tolakandro ny 12 Septambra. Ny tafika Ottoman dia nikorontana tanteraka ka nandositra. Kara Mustafa Pasha dia maty nonangonan'ny didin'i Sultan Mehmed IV — ny valiny orina Ottoman tamin'ny tsy fahombiazana ny fampahanana izay noheverina, teo amin'ny lafiny roa, ho fifanandranana rafitry ny sivilizasiona.

Ny famolavolana ny fifanandranana nataon'i Sobieski dia ara-teolojika mazava tsara. Ny taratasy nalefany tany amin'i Papa Innocent XI taorian'ny ady dia nampiasa ny tatitry ny Caesar momba ny fampahanana Galika: Venimus, vidimus, Deus vicit — "Tonga izahay, hitanay, Andriamanitra no nandresy." Ny ady dia noheverin'ny Eoropa Kristiana tsy ho fandresena jeopolitika fa ho fitehirizana providensialy ny Christendom — ny fidiran'ny fiarovan'Andriamanitra naseho amin'ny alalan'ny fahavononan'ny miaramila Kristiana hametraka ny tenany eo anelanelan'ny fandrosoana Islamika sy ny fon'ny sivilizasiona Kristianina Eropeana.

Ny vokan'ny 1683 dia farany tamin'ny sehatra stratejika. Ny fialan'ny Ottoman tao Vienna dia narahin'ny Treaty of Karlowitz tamin'ny 1699, izay ny Ottoman Empire dia nanolotra ny Hungary, Transylvania, sy ampahany lehibe ny Balkans ho ny Habsburg Empire — ny fiovana faritra Islamika voalohany fihemorana nanoherana ny Christendom Eropeana nandritra ny arivo taona eo ho eo. Ny vokany demografika sy orina ny fandresen'ny Ottoman mahomby an'i Vienna — sy ny Islamization nanaraka ny Eoropa Afovoany amin'ny alalan'ny fitaovana dhimmi izay efa niasa tao Syria, Ejipta, Afrika Afovoany, sy Anatolia — dia nohesorina tamin'ny vidin'ny ain'ny lehilahy niady sy maty teny amin'ny hantsana Kahlenberg.

Ny filaharana arivo taona hatramin'i Yarmouk ka hatramin'i Vienna dia manome fanapahan-kevitra ara-tantara tokana mitohy: na aiza na aiza ny herin'ny ara-miaramila Islamika ampy hanoherana ny fanoherana Kristiana, ny Kristianisma dia nandao amin'ny maha-fanatrehan'ny demografika miaina sy orina nandritra ny taranaka maro nanaraka. Na aiza na aiza ny herin'ny ara-miaramila Kristiana ampy hanohitra ny fandrosoana Islamika, ny Kristianisma dia velona. Ny fifandraisana dia tsy voajanahary. Maneho ny lojikan'ny rafitra voarakitra ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 1 — izay natao mba hamokatra ity vokatra ity.

VII. Ny Fototry ny Tsy Fitoviana Ara-toe-tsaina: Nahoana ny Fifandrantana Dia Tsy Ho Momba ny Politika Fotsiny

Ny famakiana ara-tantara mitsipaka ny fifandrantana voarakitra etsy ambony mety hamitaka ny antony any amin'ny jeopolitika, ny fifaninana amin'ny loharano, na ny fihevitr'ny fanitarana imperial. Nisy ireo antony ireo. Saingy ny famakiana izay mampidina ny fifanenana Islamika-Kristiana ho antony politika mitsipaka dia mandika diso ny filazan'ny olona roa momba ny tenany sy izay manamarina ny antontan'asa fa nino izay nataony.

Ny mpitari-tafika Arabo izay niampita ho any amin'ny Palestine Byzantine tamin'ny 634 dia tsy nampandehanin'ny filàna tany fambolena voalohany indrindra, na dia nanan-danja ara-stratejia aza ny fahavitan-tsakafo ny Levant. Nampandehanin'ny fianianana ara-teolojika izy ireo — voarakitra tamin'ny filazany voarakitra sy miorina amin'ny lahatsoratra Koranika nodinihina tao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 1 — fa ny fanitarana ny fitantanana Islamika nanoherana ny mponina tsy Silamo dia adidy ara-pivavahana. Ny fizarana ny tontolo ho Dar al-Islam (ny Tranon'ny Fanoloran-tena) sy Dar al-Harb (ny Tranon'ny Ady) dia tsy métaphore. Sokajy ara-dalàna izy io izay namaritana ny toe-javatra maharitra ny fifandraisana eo amin'ny fahefana politika Islamika sy ny mponina tsy Silamo mandra-pahatongan'ireo farany eo ambanin'ny fitantanana Islamika.

Mibaribary eto ny tsy fitoviana fototra eo amin’ny fomba fisainana. Ny Kristianisma, ao anatin’ireo lovam-pinoana teolojika lehibe hatramin’i Augustin ka hatramin’i Aquinas sy ny taty aoriana, dia tsy mandidy ny fanekena ara-politika ho an’ny tsy Kristiana. Ny "Iraka Lehibe" (Matthew 28:19–20) dia mandidy ny fitoriana ny Filazantsara — saingy tsy mandidy ny fampiharana ny lalàna Kristiana amin’ny mponina tsy Kristiana amin’ny alalan’ny fitaovam-piadiana ho toy ny adidy ara-pivavahana. Ny fanavahana ny fahefana ara-panahy sy ny fahefana ara-politika — ilay rafitra hoe "aloavy ho an’i Kaisara izay an’i Kaisara" (Matio 22:21) — dia namorona toerana ho an’ny fandaminana politika marolafy. Ny fitsipika Islamika klasika kosa, izay mampitambatra ny fahefana ara-pivavahana sy ara-politika ao anatin’ny foto-kevitry ny Kalifata, dia tsy manaiky izany fisarahana izany.

Ny Islam, araka ny fanazavana klasika, dia tsy manaiky ny fizarana ara-nety eo amin'ny fahefana ara-pivavahana sy ara-politika. Ny Ummah — ny fiaraha-monina ny mpino — dia mpiray tsikombakomba ara-pivavahana sy ara-politika niaraka. Ny fitantanana azy dia tsy voalohan'ny fanambarana masina fotsiny; noforonina amin'ny alany. Ny mponina tsy Silamo miaina eo ambanin'ny fitantanana Islamika dia tsy olom-pirenena ao amin'ny rafitra politika marolafy. Izy ireo dia dhimmis — olom-panjakana azo ekena izay ny sata sivily azy ireo dia voamarika amin'ny fahalemen'izy ireo ara-pivavahana ary ny fijanonany dia mifototra amin'ny fandoavany ny Jizya sy ny fanekeny ny fameperana izay amaritry ny lalàna Islamika klasika azy ireo.

Ireo rafitra roa ireo — iray izay misaraka ara-tsaina ny fahefana ara-pivavahana sy ara-politika ary iray izay mampiray azy ireo — dia tsy afaka miara-miaina amin'ny fepetra mitovy ao anaty ny sehatra politika iray. Tsy maintsy hanolo ny iray amin'ny iray farany. Ny efatra ambin'ny folo taonjato ny fifandrantana ara-miaramila sy ara-sivilizasiona voarakitra ao amin'ity lahatsoratra ity no antontan'asa ara-tantara ny zavatra mitranga rehefa nihaona amin'ny herin'ny miaramila amin'ny maha-mpitsara ny fifandraisana ireo rafitra roa ireo.

Ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina dia tsy, tsarovana indray, raharaha ny Silamo tsirairay tsy afaka miara-miaina am-pilaminana amin'ny Kristiana tsirairay. Silamo sy Kristiana an-tapitrisany no niaina amin'ny maha-mpiaramonina, mpiara-miasa, sy namana nandritra ny tantara sy manerana izao tontolo izao ankehitriny. Ny tsy fitoviana ara-toe-tsaina dia rafitra sy orina: eo amin'ny teolojia politika Islamika klasika ny califat sy ny fitaovany ara-dalàna, sy ny fisian'ny Kristianisma amin'ny maha-sivilizasiona afaka, voanerana ampahibemaso, demografika manan-danja. Na aiza na aiza ny voalohany nanana fahefana orina feno, ny farany dia tsy misy intsony.

VIII. Ny Lafiny Tsy Mifarana: Ny Fifandrantana Izay Tsy Vita

Ny Treaty of Karlowitz tamin'ny 1699 sy ny fihenan'ny herin'ny ara-miaramila Ottoman nanaraka dia namolavola vanim-potoana izay ny fifandrantana ara-sivilizasiona Islamika-Kristiana dia niseho, tamin'ny masontsivana Tandrefana, ho voavaha tamin'ny fihavianan'ny heriny Eropeana maharitra. Ny vanim-potoana colonial dia nampitomby io fiheverana io: ny fahefana Eropeana dia nampihatra ny fifehezan'ny politika tamin'ny faritany Islamika manandratra hatramin'i Afrika Afovoany Andrefana ka hatramin'i Azia Atsimo-Atsinanana, ary ny tontolon'ny Islamika dia niseho ho nolaminana farany tamin'ny fitondrana politika sy ara-miaramila Tandrefana.

Ity fiheverana ity dia ambany ara-tantara hatrany hatrany. Ny baiko ara-teolojika voarakitra ao amin'ny Quran 9:5 sy 9:29 dia tsy nofoanan'ny resy ara-miaramila. Ny foto-kevitry ny Dar al-Harb dia tsy noravana tamin'ny vanim-potoana colonial. Ny lovam-pinoana fitsipika Islamika klasika izay namaritana ny fifandraisana eo amin'ny fanjakana Islamika sy ny mponina tsy Silamo dia tsy nanao ny karazana fanavaozana ara-teolojia rafitra izay ho ilaina mba hanaovana azy ho mifanaraka amin'ny fandaminana politika marolafy ny demokrasia liberaly. Izay niova dia ny fifandanjana ny herin'ny ara-miaramila sy ara-politika, tsy ny rafitra ara-fotopampianarana izay namokatra ny fifandrantana.

Ny taonjato faha-20 sy faha-21 dia nanao izany ho hita tsara amin'ny fahamarinana vaovao. Ny fianjerany ny califat Ottoman tamin'ny 1924 dia tsy namokatra fifanaraham-pitandroana maharitra eo amin'ny Islam sy ny maodernite politika Tandrefana, fa taonjato iray ny fifadinana maharitra momba ny ho amin'ny teolojia politika Islamika — amin'ny fanavaozany maoderina isan-karazany, hatramin'ny Muslim Brotherhood ka hatramin'ny Salafism ka hatramin'ny Islamic State — hanolo ny tenany amin'ny maha-rafitra fitantanana ny fiarahamonina manandratra Silamo. Ny endrika amin'io fifadinana io dia hita ao amin'ny antontan'asa ara-tantara nodinihina ao amin'ity lahatsoratra ity: na aiza na aiza ny hetsika politika Islamika nahazo hery ampy hampihatra ny rafiny amin'ny vitsy an'isa ara-pivavahana, ny fihevitr'ny rafitra dhimmi dia niseho indray amin'ny endrika orina novana.

Ny mponina Kristiana any Iraq, Syria, Egipta, sy Nigeria — hilazana ireo tranga voarakitra indrindra ankehitriny fotsiny — dia nandritra ny faran'ny taonjato faha-20 sy ny fiandohan'ny taonjato faha-21 naharitra fianjerana demografika izay mitovy amin'ny, amin'ny endrika voavontana, ny endrika voarakitra ho an'ny Syria tamin'ny taonjato faha-7 sy ny Afovoany Afriky tamin'ny taonjato faha-8. Ny mponina Kristiana any Iraq, noheverina ho eo ho eo 1.5 tapitrisa tamin'ny 2003, dia efa nihena tamin'ny 300,000 tamin'ny 2015 taorian'ny fiakaran'ny Islamic State sy ny taona maro nitrangan'ny herisetra manavaka. Ny fiaraha-monina Coptic an'i Egipta — ny mpandova demografika mivantana ny sivilizasiona Kristiana izay namokatra an'i Origen sy Athanasius — dia miatrika fandrarana orina maharitra, herisetra be vola matetika, sy fampihenana demografika miadana izay mitohy ankehitriny.

Ny fifandrantana dia tsy vita. Tsy nosoloany rafitra vaovao ny marolafy teolojia marina ao anatin'ny lovam-pinoana Islamika. Mitohy araka ny lojika rafitra mitovy izay nanehoany nandritra ny telo ambin'ny folo taonjato: ny lojika rafitra teolojia izay tsy manaiky ny maharitry ny maha-ara-nety ny Kristianisma ho sivilizasiona mitovy, ary izay matetika mamokatra tsindry orina manoloana ny fihenanan'ny demografika Kristiana na aiza na aiza hahazoana fitondrana ara-politika.

Ny fanekena izany dia tsy antso amin'ny fahavalo amin'ny Silamo tsirairay na fiaraha-monina Silamo. Ny fikitihana amin'ny fahamarinana ara-tantara momba izay haseho ny antontan'asa — ary amin'ny fanapahan-kevitra mifanaraka fa ny fitehirizana ny Kristianisma ho sivilizasiona miaina dia mitaky ny karazany fandalinana ny fandonana izay miatrika io izay naneho i Charles Martel tao Tours, izay naneho i Constantine XI teny amin'ny rindrin'i Constantinople, ary izay naneho i John III Sobieski teny amin'ny hantsana Kahlenberg.

Ny Ecclesia Militans — nodinihina ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 2 — dia tsy sisa tavela taonjato maromaro lasa. Izy io ny toe-javatra voaseho ara-tantara ny fahaveloman'ny Kristianisma ao amin'ny tontolon'izany ahitana teolojia politika miorina amin'ny fanoloran-tena amin'ny fanoloran-tenany. Ny safidy hafa amin'ny fahatakarana izany dia tsy fiara-miaina am-pilaminana. Izy io no anjaran'ny Kristiana any Afovoany Afriky, any Anatolia, any Mesopotamia: fiaraha-monina izay tsy velona tamin'ny fifanenana noho tsy fiomananany ho amin'ny zava-misy ny fifanenana.

IX. Madagasikara: Firenena Kristiana ao amin'ny Tontolon'izay Mitohy ny Fifandrantana

Madagasikara dia maneho ny tenany, ara-lalàmpanorenana, ho fanjakana mitsipaka. Ny lalàmpanorenan'ny dia manome antoka ny fahalalahana ara-pivavahana ary mandrara ny fampiorenana finoana fanjakana. Amin'ny fampiharana, Madagasikara dia firenena Kristiana manandratra: eo anelanelan'ny 80 sy 85 isan-jato ny mponina dia manaiky ho Kristiana, ary ny fitaomana ny — voalohany indrindra Protestanta sy Katolika — orina amin'ny fiainan'ny ampahibemaso Malagasy, ny fanabeazana, sy ny kolontsaina sivily dia manerana sy miorim-paka ara-tantara, araka ny nodinihina ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 2.

Ity fitambaran'ity — ny mitsipaka ara-lalàmpanorenana amin'ny maha-izy ara-sivilizasiona Kristiana de facto — dia tsy tsy mahazatra amin'ny tontolon'ankehitriny. Saingy mitondra ny vokany izay ny antontan'asa ara-tantara nodinihina ao amin'ity lahatsoratra ity no mahatonga tsy azo tsidiho. Sivilizasiona izay Kristiana amin'ny fombolantongotra ara-kolontsaina saingy mitsipaka amin'ny fahatakarana ny tenany orina dia sivilizasiona izay efa nikidika ny tenany ara-teolojia ampahany. Mitehiry ny lovan'ny Kristianisma ara-kolontsaina izy io tsy misy ny rafitra ara-teolojia — ny lovam-pinoana Ecclesia Militans — izay ara-tantara namokatra sy niaro izany lova izany. Izy io, amin'ity fomba ity, dia miaina amin'ny renivola tsy novokariany mavitrika intsony.

Ny vitsy an'isa Silamo Madagasikara — miorim-paka any amin'ny faritra amoron-tsiraka avaratra andrefana sy ao amin'ny fiaraha-monina Indo-Pakistani sy Comorian — dia tsy, ankehitriny, herin'ny politika mitady ny karazana fiovana orina izay hanelingelina ny toetrany ara-sivilizasiona Kristiana ny firenena. Ny fandonana avy hatrany amin'ny Kristianisma Malagasy dia tsy tsindry ara-miaramila Islamika. Izy io ny fandonana mitovy izay miatrika ny Kristianisma nanerana ny tontolon'ankehitriny: tsy fahalalana ara-teolojia, fifanaraham-pivavahana orina, sy ny fihenan-drihitra ny fianianana fa ny finoana dia mendrika hiaro amin'ny vidiny rehetra.

Saingy ny antontan'asa ara-tantara dia mitaky ny Kristiana Malagasy hahatakatra ny tontolon'ny ara-sivilizasiona malalaka izay iainany. Ny resaka nanomboka tao Ajnadayn tamin'ny 634 sy nitohy tamin'i Yarmouk, Alexandria, Carthage, Tours, Constantinople, Lepanto, sy Vienna dia tsy vita. Mitohy ankehitriny any Iraq, any Ejipta, any Nigeria, any Sahel — ary hatramin'ny tokony hahatongavany amin'ny zorony rehetra ny tontolo izay miara-misy ny Kristianisma sy ny Islam politika, noho ny lojika rafitra ny rafitra teolojika izay mampandeha azy dia tsy niova.

Ny maha-Kristiana Madagasikara dia noforonina, araka ny voarakitra ao amin'ny Lahatsoratra 2, tamin'ny fanenjehana. Ny martioran'i Ambohipotsy dia nifidy fahafatesana fa tsy fandavana ny finoana teo ambanin'i Ranavalona I. Io safidy io dia tsy voajanahary tamin'ny kolontsaina na ny toetr'olombelona. Fanehoana fianianana izy io — fa ny finoana dia sarobidy kokoa noho ny fahavelomana tsy misy azy — izay namokatra ny lovam-pinoana Ecclesia Militans tamin'ny taonjato rehetra sy ny tontolo rehetra izay voasendrin'ny Kristianisma tokoa. Io fianianana io ihany no nitehiry ny Kristianisma tao Tours, tao Lepanto, tao Vienna.

Ny fanontaniana ho an'ny Kristiana Malagasy — toy ny Kristiana manerana izao tontolo izao amin'ny taonjato faha-21 — dia ny fisian'io fianianana io mbola velona. Na ny finoana noraisina ho lova avy amin'ny martioran'i Ambohipotsy sy ireo mpiaro an'i Vienna sy ny monks Coptic any Ejipta dia azo hazava amin'ny maha-zavatra mendrika hiaro amin'ny fahamazavana sy ny herim-po mitovy izay nitaky ny fitehirizana azy ara-tantara.

Ny tantara dia tsy manome valiny mora ho an'io fanontaniana io. Manome izao fotsiny: ny sivilizasiona nahatakatra izay niaro, ary vonona niaro azy, dia velona. Ireo tsy nanao izany, dia veloma. Ny safidy dia tsy eo amin'ny fifandrantana sy ny fiadanana. Ny safidy dia eo amin'ny fahamazavana sy ny fahamatorana. Ary ny fahamatorana, amin'ity fifanenana ara-sivilizasiona manokana ity, dia manana vidiny ara-tantara voarakitra.
