Why Without The Church Militantism (readiness to defend the faith on both physical and spiritual fronts), Christianity Would Have Died A Long Time Ago

From the book Fear God and Take Your Own Part by Theodore Roosevelt (New York, George H. Doran Company, 1915), Chapter VI – "Peace Insurance by Preparedness Against War," p. 196–197:

"Christianity is not the creed of Asia and Africa at this moment solely because the seventh century Christians of Asia and Africa, in addition to being rent asunder among themselves by bitter sectarian animosities—and sectarian intolerance and animosity stand for most that is evil in Christianity—had trained themselves not to fight, whereas the Moslems were trained to fight. Christianity was saved in Europe solely because the peoples of Europe fought. If the peoples of Europe in the seventh and eighth centuries, and on up to and including the seventeenth century, had not possessed a military equality with, and gradually a growing superiority over, the Mohammedans who invaded Europe, Europe would at this moment be Mohammedan, and the Christian religion would be exterminated. Wherever the Mohammedans have had complete sway, wherever the Christians have been unable to resist them by the sword, Christianity has ultimately disappeared. From the hammer of Charles Martel in the battle of Tours 732 A.D. to the sword of Sobieski in the siege of Vienna in 1683, Christianity owed its safety in Europe to the fact that it was able to show that it could and would fight as well as the Mohammedans aggressors."

I. Introduction: The Problem of Historical Memory

Theodore Roosevelt's observation is not primarily a military argument. It is, at its core, a historical and theological one: that the survival of any religious tradition depends not only on its doctrinal content, but on the willingness of its adherents to actively defend it — both in the realm of the spirit and, when necessity demands, in the realm of physical reality. This essay examines that claim and argues that the concept of the Ecclesia Militans — the Church Militant — is not a medieval aberration, but a theological and historical necessity without which Christianity would not exist today.

This argument is advanced with particular attention to the Malagasy Christian tradition, which offers one of the most striking illustrations of the principle: a community that survived not because it was left unchallenged, but because it chose, at profound personal cost, to endure.

II. Theological Foundation: The Church Militant in Scripture and Tradition

The concept of the Ecclesia Militans — the Church as a body engaged in active spiritual warfare — is not a medieval invention. Its roots are Pauline. In his second letter to the Corinthians, Paul declares: "For though we walk in the flesh, we do not war according to the flesh. For the weapons of our warfare are not carnal but mighty in God for pulling down strongholds" (2 Cor. 10:3–4). The letter to the Ephesians similarly calls believers to put on the full armor of God and to stand against spiritual forces of darkness (Eph. 6:11–17).

The patristic tradition developed this theology with considerable rigor. Origen of Alexandria, writing in the third century, distinguished between the spiritual warfare incumbent upon all Christians and the physical dimension that historical circumstances could make necessary. Augustine of Hippo, in his doctrine of the bellum iustum (just war), provided the theological framework by which physical defense could be understood not as a contradiction of Christian love, but as one of its possible expressions — the protection of the innocent and the preservation of conditions under which the faith could continue to flourish. Thomas Aquinas further systematized this in the Summa Theologica, articulating three conditions for a morally licit use of force: legitimate authority, just cause, and right intention. The point is not that physical conflict is desirable. The point is that Christian theology has never equated passivity with virtue, nor surrender with holiness.

III. The Seventh Century: The Fragility of Established Christian Territories

Roosevelt's observation draws its force from a well-documented historical record. The rapid expansion of Islamic military power in the seventh century did not primarily displace pagan populations. It displaced deeply rooted Christian ones. Syria, the birthplace of early monasticism and home to the Council of Antioch, fell in 636. Egypt, where Origen and Athanasius had worked, fell by 641. North Africa — the land of Tertullian, Cyprian, and Augustine himself — was conquered by the end of the seventh century.

These were not peripheral territories of a nascent faith. They were its intellectual and spiritual heartlands. The disappearance of Christianity from these regions was not gradual theological erosion. It was the consequence of military defeat followed by sustained administrative and social pressure that, over generations, rendered Christian practice untenable for most inhabitants. Where Christians could not or did not defend themselves — physically or institutionally — the faith did not survive in any demographically significant form.

IV. Europe's Survival: A Case Study in Militant Christianity

The contrast with Europe is instructive. At the Battle of Tours in 732, Charles Martel halted the northward advance of Umayyad forces into Frankish territory — an engagement that Edward Gibbon, in his Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire, considered decisive for the preservation of Western Christendom. Nearly a millennium later, at the Siege of Vienna in 1683, the combined forces of the Holy League under John III Sobieski of Poland repelled the Ottoman advance into Central Europe.

These were not merely geopolitical events. They were moments at which the physical survival of Christian civilization in Europe was genuinely at stake. The faith survived in the West not because it was philosophically superior or because providence intervened without human agency, but because particular men and communities were prepared to defend it at the cost of their lives. The institutional Church, the monasteries that preserved classical learning, the theological schools that shaped Western civilization — all of these depended, at critical junctures, on the willingness of Christian communities to resist conquest by force of arms.

V. The Spiritual Front: Martyrdom, Doctrine, and the Inner Militia

The Church Militant encompasses far more than military resistance. The greater and more constant front has always been spiritual. From the earliest centuries, when Christians faced Roman persecution, the Church survived through what Tertullian identified as the paradoxical power of martyrdom: semen est sanguis Christianorum — the blood of Christians is seed.

This spiritual militancy took multiple forms: the doctrinal battles of the early councils, where figures such as Athanasius of Alexandria stood against Arianism at significant personal and institutional cost; the labors of monastic copyists who preserved the Scriptures and patristic texts through periods of social collapse; and the work of apologists and theologians who engaged philosophical challenges to the faith with intellectual rigor rather than retreat. The survival of orthodox Christian doctrine was not automatic. It required men and women who understood that the faith was worth defending — and who acted accordingly.

VI. The Malagasy Case: Ranavalona I and the Triumph of Spiritual Militancy

The history of Christianity in Madagascar provides one of the most compelling illustrations of the principle under examination. Between 1835 and 1861, Queen Ranavalona I imposed a systematic prohibition on Christian practice, expelling foreign missionaries and subjecting Malagasy converts to severe legal penalties including imprisonment, forced labor, and execution.

What is historically remarkable is not that persecution occurred — it occurred in many contexts — but that the Malagasy Christian community survived it without the support of institutional structures, foreign protection, or armed resistance. Believers memorized Scripture to preserve it when physical texts were confiscated. They organized clandestine worship with full awareness of the legal consequences of discovery. They chose death rather than apostasy — the martyrs of Ambohipotsy, executed between 1837 and 1849, being among the most documented instances of this conviction.

This was not passive endurance. It was active, deliberate, and costly spiritual militancy. When Ranavalona I died in 1861 and her successor Radama II lifted the prohibition, Christianity did not re-emerge as a fragile remnant. It had, under persecution, grown in both depth of conviction and in numbers. The Malagasy case thus demonstrates with particular clarity that the Church Militant is not a concept foreign to Malagasy Christian experience. It is, in fact, part of its own foundational history.

VII. Militancy as an Expression of Love, Not Its Contradiction

A common misreading of the Church Militant concept conflates militancy with violence, or interprets the call to defense as incompatible with the Christian commandment of love. Augustine's framework of the just war was constructed precisely to address this tension. The defense of one's community and one's faith — when pursued with right intention and proportionate means — is not a contradiction of love. It is love expressed under conditions of threat. Thomas Aquinas argued further that the willingness to permit evil out of a misapplied notion of non-resistance is not holiness but negligence.

The martyrs did not resist their persecutors with violence. They resisted the demand to renounce truth. That refusal — costly, deliberate, sustained — is the purest expression of spiritual militancy. It declares that there are things worth more than comfort and survival, and that the faith is among them.

VIII. Contemporary Implications

The contemporary relevance of this argument is not primarily military. The threats that face Christianity today are predominantly intellectual, cultural, and moral: the erosion of theological literacy within Christian communities, the marginalization of religious conviction in public life, and the internal pressure of doctrinal compromise for the sake of social acceptability.

The Church Militant tradition responds to each of these with the same principle that animated Athanasius, the Malagasy martyrs, and the defenders of Vienna alike: that the faith is worth defending, that its defense requires preparation and courage, and that the alternative to active defense is not peaceful coexistence but gradual extinction. For Malagasy Christians in particular, this heritage is not borrowed from Western theological tradition alone. It is their own. The generation that kept the faith alive under Ranavalona I did not survive by accommodation. They survived by conviction.

IX. Conclusion: The Historical Verdict

The historical record examined in this essay supports a single, consistent conclusion: Christianity survived not because it was left undisturbed, but because its adherents — in diverse contexts, across many centuries — chose to defend it at great personal cost, on both the spiritual and, when necessary, the physical front.

Roosevelt's observation is not an invitation to belligerence. It is a call to historical honesty. The Church Militant is the Church as it has actually existed in history — not as an institution of comfortable sentiment, but as a community of conviction capable of costly defense. Without that willingness, the faith that Malagasy Christians inherited, and that Christians across the world profess, would not exist.

To acknowledge this is not to glorify violence. It is to take seriously the price that was paid — in lives, in suffering, in sustained courage — for the faith that is now received as inheritance. And to receive an inheritance seriously is to understand what it cost, and to consider what one is willing to pay to pass it on.

I. Fampidirana: Ny olan'ny fanadinoana ny tantara

Ny fanamarihana nataon'i Theodore Roosevelt dia tsy hevitra ara-miaramila voalohany indrindra. Ao am-pony dia hevitra ara-tantara sy ara-teolojia: ny fahaveloman'ny finoana dia miankina tsy amin'ny votoatiny am-pinoana ihany, fa amin'ny fahavononan'ny mpino hiaro izany — na eo amin'ny sehatra ara-panahy, na eo amin'ny zava-misy ara-batana raha takian'ny toe-javatra. Ity lahatsoratra ity dia mandinika izany filazana izany ary manamafy fa ny foto-kevitry ny Ecclesia Militans — ny Fiangonana Mpiady — dia tsy fivilian-dàlana tamin'ny Moyen Âge, fa filàna ara-tantara sy ara-teolojia tsy maintsy nisy raha toa ny Kristianisma no mbola misy ankehitriny.

Ity hevitra ity dia alefa amin'ny fijery manokana ny lovan'ny Kristianina malagasy — izay manolotra iray amin'ireo fanoharana manainga indrindra: fiaraha-monina izay velona tsy noho izy navelan'ny mpanenjika, fa noho izy nifidy, tamin'ny vidiny lehibe manokana, ny haharetana.

II. Fototra ara-teolojia: Ny Fiangonana Mpiady ao amin'ny Soratra Masina sy ny Lovam-pinoana

Ny foto-kevitry ny Ecclesia Militans — ny Fiangonana amin'ny maha-fiaraha-monina miady ady ara-panahy — dia tsy hevitra nateraky ny Moyen Âge. Ny fototry ny zavatra dia ao amin'ny epistily Pauliniana. Ao amin'ny taratasy faharoa nataony ho an'ny Korintiana, i Paoly dia milaza hoe: "Fa na dia mandeha ao amin'ny nofo aza izahay, dia tsy miady araka ny nofo. Fa ny fiadiana anay dia tsy fiadiana araka ny nofo, fa mahery amin'Andriamanitra hanapotehana ny fiarovana" (2 Kor. 10:3–4). Toy izany koa ny taratasy ho an'ny Efesiana, izay miantso ny mpino hampiasa ny fitaovam-piadiana feno avy amin'Andriamanitra mba hanoherana ny herin'ny maizina (Efes. 6:11–17).

Ny lovam-pinoana taorian'ny Apostoly dia namolavola izany teolojia izany tamin'ny henjana lehibe. I Origène avy any Aleksandria, nanoratra tamin'ny taonjato fahatelo, dia nampisaraka ny ady ara-panahy izay adidy ho an'ny Kristianina rehetra sy ny lafiny ara-batana izay mety ho takian'ny toe-javatra. I Augustin avy any Hippo, tamin'ny alalan'ny fampianarana azy momba ny bellum iustum (ady marina), dia nanome ny rafitra ara-teolojia izay ahafantarana fa ny fiarovana ara-batana dia tsy mifanipaka amin'ny fitiavana kristiana, fa mety ho iray amin'ireo endrika fitenenana izany — ny fiarovana ny mahantra sy ny fitehirizana ny toe-java-tsoa izay ahafahan'ny finoana mitohy maniry. Nandraiketitra izany i Tomà Akinasy ao amin'ny Summa Theologica, nanoritsoritra fepetra telo momba ny fampiasana hery ara-pitondrantena: fahefana ara-dalàna, antony marina, ary tanjona tsara. Tsy ny fifandirana no tiana eto. Fa ny zavatra tiana ampahafantarana dia izao: tsy nampitovy ny fipetrapetrahan-tena amin'ny hatsaram-panahy ny teolojia kristiana, na ny fanolorana tena amin'ny fahamarinan-tena.

III. Ny Taonjato Faha-7: Ny fahalemen'ny Tany Kristiana efa Miorina

Ny hery amin'ny fanamarihana nataon'i Roosevelt dia avy amin'ny antontan'isa ara-tantara voarakitra tsara. Ny fielezan'ny herin'ny tafika silamo tamin'ny taonjato faha-7 dia tsy namindra ny mponina jentilisa voalohany indrindra. Izy io dia namindra ireo mponina kristiana izay efa niorim-paka lalina. I Syria, fiavian'ny fihemorana teo am-boalohany sy tranon'ny Konsily an'i Antiokia, dia lavo tamin'ny 636. I Egypta, izay niasàn'i Origène sy Atanazy, dia lavo tamin'ny 641. I Afovoany Afriky — tany i Tertoliana, Sipiriana, ary i Augustin tenany — dia nobaboina tamin'ny faran'ny taonjato faha-7.

Ireny dia tsy tany an-tsisiny ho an'ny finoana vao nanomboka. Ireny no foiben'ny saina sy ny fanahy. Ny fandrarana ny Kristianisma tany amin'ireo faritra ireny dia tsy fahasimban'ny teolojia nindramindrana. Vokatry ny resy ara-miaramila izany, narahin'ny tsindry ara-pitantanana sy ara-tsosialy maharitra izay, nandritra ny taranaka maro, nahatonga ny fanaovana Kristianisma ho tsy azon'ny ankamaroan'ny mponina atao. Tany amin'izay tsy afaka na tsy nety niaro tena ny Kristianina — ara-batana na ara-orina — dia tsy velona tamin'ny endrika demografika lehibe ny finoana.

IV. Ny Fahaveloman'i Eoropa: Fanadihadiana ny Kristianisma Mpiady

Mampianatra zavatra ny tsy fitoviana amin'i Eoropa. Tamin'ny Ady tao Tours tamin'ny 732, i Charles Martel dia nisakana ny fandrosoana avaratra an'ny tafika Umayyad ho any amin'ny faritra Frankia — fifanandrinana izay noheveran'i Edouard Gibbon, ao amin'ny Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire, ho manapaka ho an'ny fitehirizana ny Kristianisma tandrefana. Teo amin'ny faran'ny arivo taona taorian'izay, tamin'ny Fahirano an'i Vienne tamin'ny 1683, ny tafika miaraka tao amin'ny Holy League notarihin'i John III Sobieski avy any Pologne dia nandroaka ny fandrosoan'ny Ottomana ho any Eoropa Afovoany.

Ireny dia tsy zava-nitranga ara-jeopolitika fotsiny. Ireny no fotoana izay nampidi-doza tokoa ny fahaveloman'ny sivilizasiona kristiana tany Eoropa. Ny finoana dia velona tany Andrefana tsy noho izy ambony ara-filozofia, na noho ny Fanamparitahan'Andriamanitra nandraharaha tsy nampanao asa ny olombelona, fa noho ny lehilahy sy ny fiaraha-monina vonona niaro azy tamin'ny vidiny ny ainy. Ny Fiangonana orina, ny monastera izay nitehiry ny fahalalana fahiny, ny sekoly teolojia izay namolavola ny sivilizasiona tandrefana — ireo rehetra ireo dia niankina, tamin'ny fotoana manapaka, tamin'ny fahavononan'ny fiaraha-monina kristiana hanoherana ny fanjanahan-tany tamin'ny heriny.

V. Ny Sehatra ara-Panahy: Martyrolojia, Fotopampianarana, sy ny Militia Anatiny

Ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia mangarahara lavitra kokoa noho ny fanoherana ara-miaramila. Ny sehatra lehibe sy maharitra kokoa dia ara-panahy hatrany. Hatramin'ny taonjato voalohany indrindra, fony ny Kristianina niatrika fanenjehana Romana, ny Fiangonana dia velona tamin'ny alalan'izay nambaran'i Tertoliana ho hery mifanohitra amin'ny masontsivana amin'ny martyrolojia: semen est sanguis Christianorum — ny ran'ny Kristianina dia voa.

Io fijoroana ara-panahy io dia niseho tamin'ny endrika maro: ny ady momba ny fotopampianarana tamin'ny Konsily voalohany, izay nijoro tamin'izay i Atanazy avy any Aleksandria nanoherana ny Arianisma tamin'ny vidiny manokana sy orina; ny asa ny mpikopy monaka izay nitehiry ny Soratra Masina sy ny soratra taorian'ny Apostoly nandritra ny vanim-potoana fandroboan'ny fiarahamonina; ary ny asan'ireo apologista sy teolojiana izay niatrika ny fanamafisana ara-filozofia ho an'ny finoana tamin'ny henjana ara-tsaina fa tsy fihemorana. Ny fahaveloman'ny fotopampianarana kristiana ara-dalàna dia tsy ho azy. Nitaky lehilahy sy vehivavy izay nahalala fa ny finoana dia mendrika hotanterahana — ary nitombo tamin'izany.

VI. Ny Tranga Malagasy: Ranavalona I sy ny Fandresena ny Fijoroana ara-Panahy

Ny tantaran'ny Kristianisma teto Madagasikara dia manolotra iray amin'ireo fanoharana manainga indrindra ny foto-kevitra voamarika eto. Teo anelanelan'ny 1835 sy 1861, i Ranavalona I Mpanjaka dia nanapa-keloka fampiharana fady henjana tamin'ny fanaovana Kristianisma, nandroaka ireo misionera vahiny ary nanameloka ireo mpiovafo malagasy tamin'ny sazy ara-dalàna mafy anisan'izany ny fisamborana, fanompoana an-tery, sy famonoana.

Izay manaitra ara-tantara dia tsy ny nitrangan'ny fanenjehana — nitranga tamin'ny toe-javatra maro izany — fa ny nahaveloman'ny fiaraha-monina kristiana malagasy azy izany tsy nisy ny fanohanana ny rafitra orina, fiarovana vahiny, na fanoherana ara-miaramila. Ny mpino dia nanonona ny Soratra Masina mba hitehirizana azy rehefa nogiazan'ny mpanapaka ny lahatsoratra ara-batana. Nikarakara fanompoam-pivavahana mangingina izy ireo tamin'ny fahalalana feno ny vokatra ara-dalàna raha hita. Nifidy ny fahafatesana mihoatra ny fandavana ny finoana izy ireo — ny martiora tao Ambohipotsy, novonoina teo anelanelan'ny 1837 sy 1849, no anisan'ireo tranga voarakitra tsara indrindra amin'izany finoana izany.

Tsy fipetrapetrahan-tena fotsiny izany. Fijoroana ara-panahy mavitrika, voaanja-mafy, sy nandany vidiny izany. Rehefa maty i Ranavalona I tamin'ny 1861 ary nanaisotra ny fady ny mpandimby azy Radama II, ny Kristianisma dia tsy nipoitra ho sisa tavela malemy. Izy io, teo ambany fanenjehana, dia nitombo na amin'ny halalina ny finoana na amin'ny isan'ny mpino. Ny tranga malagasy dia mampiseho mazava tsara fa ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia tsy foto-kevitra vahiny amin'ny traikefan'ny Kristianina malagasy. Izy io, amin'ny tena maha-izy azy, dia ampahany amin'ny tantarany fototra — ny tantaran'ny razambenao.

VII. Ny Fijoroana ho Fanehoana ny Fitiavana, fa tsy Mifanipaka aminy

Ny fandikan-kevitra mahazatra ny foto-kevitry ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia mampifangaro ny fijoroana amin'ny herisetra, na manazava fa ny antson'ny fiarovana dia mifanipaka amin'ny didin'ny fitiavana ao amin'ny Kristianisma. Ny rafitry ny ady marina nataon'i Augustin dia natao mba hiresahana ity fifanatrehan'ny hevitra ity. Ny fiarovana ny fiaraha-monin'ny olona sy ny finoany — raha tanterahina amin'ny tanjona marina sy ny fomba mifanentana — dia tsy mifanipaka amin'ny fitiavana. Fitiavana aseho ao anatin'ny toe-javatra mampidi-doza izany. I Tomà Akinasy dia nandalina fa ny fahavononana hampandroaka ny ratsy avy amin'ny fahatakarana diso ny tsy fanoherana dia tsy hatsaram-panahy fa fahadisoan-kevitra.

Ny martiora dia tsy nanohitra ny mpanenjehana azy tamin'ny herisetra. Nanohitra ny fitakiana handà ny fahamarinana izy ireo. Io fandavana io — nandany vidiny, voaanja, maharitra — no fanehoana madio indrindra ny fijoroana ara-panahy. Izany dia milaza fa misy zavatra sarobidy noho ny fiadanana sy ny fahavelomana, ary ny finoana no iray amin'ireny.

VIII. Ny Mifandray amin'ny Ankehitriny

Ny mifandray amin'ny ankehitriny amin'ity hevitra ity dia tsy ara-miaramila voalohany indrindra. Ny loza mananontanona ny Kristianisma ankehitriny dia ara-tsaina, ara-kolontsaina, sy ara-pitondrantena voalohany indrindra: ny fahasimban'ny fahalalana teolojia ao anatin'ny fiaraha-monina kristiana, ny fampidiriana ny finoana ara-pivavahana tsy ho an'ny besinimaro, ary ny tsindry anatiny amin'ny fifanaraham-pivavahana noho ny fitadiavana fanekena ara-tsosialy.

Ny lovam-pinoana ny Fiangonana Mpiady dia mamaly ireo rehetra ireo tamin'ny foto-kevitra mitovy izay nampandeha an'i Atanazy, ny martiora malagasy, ary ny mpiaro an'i Vienne: fa ny finoana dia mendrika hotanterahana, fa ny fiarovana azy dia mitaky fanomanana sy herim-po, ary fa ny liana-pifandraisana ho solon'ny fiarovana mavitrika dia tsy fiaraha-miaina am-pilaminana fa fandrosoan-dàlan'ny fanjavozana. Ho an'ny Kristianina malagasy manokana, ity lova ity dia tsy nindramina avy amin'ny lovam-pinoana teolojia tandrefana irery. Azy io. Ny taranaka izay nitehiry ny finoana velona teo ambany fitantanan'i Ranavalona I dia tsy velona noho ny nampifanaraka tena. Velona noho ny finoana mafy izy ireo.

IX. Fehiny: Ny Didim-pitsaran'ny Tantara

Ny antontan'asa ara-tantara voamarika amin'ity lahatsoratra ity dia manohana fanapahan-kevitra tokana, mifanaraka: ny Kristianisma dia velona tsy noho navelan'ny mpanohitra azy ho tsy voakorontana, fa noho ny mpino — tamin'ny toe-javatra isan-karazany, nandritra ny taonjato maro — nifidy hiaro azy tamin'ny vidiny lehibe, na eo amin'ny sehatra ara-panahy na, raha nila, eo amin'ny sehatra ara-batana.

Ny fanamarihana nataon'i Roosevelt dia tsy fanasana ho amin'ny fifandanjana. Antson'ny fahamarinana ara-tantara izany. Ny Fiangonana Mpiady no Fiangonana araka ny niainany ara-tantara tokoa — tsy orina amin'ny fihetseham-po mahafinaritra, fa fiaraha-monina finoana mahay miaro amin'ny vidiny. Tsy nisy izany fahavononana izany, dia tsy misy ny finoana izay nandovan'ny Kristianina malagasy, sy izay ieken'ny Kristianina maneran-tany.

Ny fanekena izany dia tsy fanandratana ny herisetra. Fandinihana mafy ny vidin'izay naloaina — tamin'ny ain'olona, tamin'ny fijaliana, tamin'ny herim-po maharitra — ho an'ny finoana izay noraisina ankehitriny ho lova.
